MUTUAL FUNDS, DEMAT ACCOUNTS, AND BROKERAGE — INVESTING THROUGH BANKS

OVERVIEW
Indian banks have evolved from pure deposit-takers to full-spectrum financial supermarkets, offering mutual fund distribution, demat accounts, broking services, IPO subscription via ASBA, insurance products, and more. Most banks act either as direct sub-broker, AMC distributor, or via group entities (e.g., HDFC Securities, ICICI Direct, Axis Direct, Kotak Securities, SBI Cap Securities). Understanding these products and channels helps customers build wealth beyond traditional deposits.

MUTUAL FUNDS — BASICS

What is a Mutual Fund?
A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests in stocks, bonds, money market instruments, or a combination, managed by a professional fund manager. Investors hold "units" representing their share of the portfolio. Returns come from capital appreciation and distributions.

Regulator: Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) governs mutual funds; the Association of Mutual Funds in India (AMFI) is the industry body.

Net Asset Value (NAV): Per-unit value of the fund, calculated daily after market close. Buy/sell units at NAV (with applicable charges).

Types of Mutual Funds:

1. EQUITY FUNDS (invest in stocks):
- Large Cap: Stable, top 100 companies (Nifty 50 index funds, large cap funds)
- Mid Cap: Companies ranked 101-250
- Small Cap: Below 250th
- Multi Cap / Flexi Cap: Across market caps
- Sectoral / Thematic: Specific sectors (banking, IT, pharma)
- ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme): 3-year lock-in, qualifies for 80C deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh — tax-saving equity fund
- Index Funds: Passive funds tracking Nifty 50, Sensex, Nifty Next 50, Nifty Bank, etc.
- ETF (Exchange Traded Fund): Listed funds tradeable on stock exchange

2. DEBT FUNDS (invest in bonds and money market):
- Liquid Funds: Ultra-short, 1-90 days; alternative to savings account
- Ultra Short Duration: 3-6 months
- Short Duration: 1-3 years
- Medium / Long Duration: 3-7 years
- Corporate Bond Funds
- Gilt Funds (government securities only)
- Credit Risk Funds (lower-rated bonds, higher yield)

3. HYBRID FUNDS (mix of equity and debt):
- Aggressive Hybrid: 65%-80% equity
- Balanced Advantage: Dynamic allocation
- Conservative Hybrid: 10%-25% equity
- Multi-Asset: Equity + debt + gold

4. SOLUTION-ORIENTED:
- Retirement Funds
- Children's Funds

5. OTHER:
- Fund of Funds
- International funds (invest in overseas stocks)
- Gold Funds (commodity-linked)

INVESTING IN MUTUAL FUNDS

Modes:
1. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): Fixed monthly amount; rupee-cost averaging; recommended for long-term equity investing
2. Lump sum: One-time large investment
3. STP (Systematic Transfer Plan): Periodic transfer between funds
4. SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan): Periodic withdrawal for income

Investment Amount:
- Minimum SIP: ₹500 per month (some funds ₹100 for select schemes)
- Minimum lump sum: ₹1,000-₹5,000
- Maximum: No upper limit (single investment ₹2 lakh+ triggers KYC review)

DIRECT vs REGULAR PLAN
- Direct Plan: Buy directly from AMC's website / SEBI-registered platform (Zerodha Coin, Groww, Kuvera, MFDirect, Paytm Money)
- Regular Plan: Through a distributor (bank, advisor) — pays 0.5%-1.5% extra annually as commission
- Long-term cost difference: Significant — 1% over 25 years can cost 25%+ in final corpus
- For self-directed investors: Always choose Direct Plan

Expense Ratio:
- Annual charge by AMC as % of assets
- Direct plan: 0.10%-1.20% typically
- Regular plan: 0.50%-2.25%
- Lower expense ratio = higher net returns

KYC FOR MUTUAL FUNDS
- One-time KYC done with CKYCR (CERSAI's Central KYC Records Registry)
- Or KRA (KYC Registration Agency) — CDSL Ventures, NSDL, CAMS-KRA, KARVY, NDML
- Documents: PAN, Aadhaar, photograph, bank proof
- Aadhaar-based e-KYC fully digital
- One-time process; valid across all AMCs

DEMAT ACCOUNT

What is a Demat Account?
"Demat" = Dematerialised. A demat account holds your stock holdings, bonds, ETFs, mutual fund units, and other securities in electronic form.

Depositories: NSDL (National Securities Depository Limited) and CDSL (Central Depository Services Limited) — two centralised depositories in India.

Depository Participant (DP): The intermediary (typically your broker or bank) that opens and maintains your demat account on behalf of NSDL/CDSL.

3-IN-1 ACCOUNT
Banks offer a 3-in-1 account that links:
1. Savings bank account (for funds)
2. Demat account (for holdings)
3. Trading account (for buy/sell orders)

The integration auto-debits and credits between accounts seamlessly. Popular 3-in-1 products:
- ICICI Direct (ICICI Bank)
- HDFC Securities (HDFC Bank)
- Axis Direct (Axis Bank)
- Kotak Securities (Kotak Bank)
- SBI Cap Securities (SBI)

Advantages:
- Convenience of one platform
- Auto-debit / auto-credit
- Bank credibility
- Customer service via branch

Disadvantages:
- Higher brokerage compared to discount brokers
- Slower platform / app vs. fintech brokers (Zerodha Kite, Groww, Upstox)

DEMAT CHARGES (INDICATIVE FY 2024-25)

Account Opening:
- Many banks now charge ₹0 (waived under promotions)
- Few still charge ₹100-₹500 + GST

Annual Maintenance Charge (AMC):
- Banks: ₹450-₹1,000 + GST
- Discount brokers (Zerodha, Groww, Upstox): ₹300-₹450 + GST
- BSDA (Basic Service Demat Account): Reduced AMC for small investors (holdings ≤ ₹4 lakh — first slab nil, second slab ₹100)

Transaction Charges:
- Debit (sell, off-market transfer): ₹5-₹25 per transaction + 18% GST + ₹13-₹25 NSDL/CDSL fee
- Credit (buy): FREE
- Off-market transfer charges: ₹10-₹30
- DIS (Delivery Instruction Slip) book: ₹50-₹150 (digital DIS now common)

BROKERAGE CHARGES (TRADING)

Bank brokers (e.g., ICICI Direct, HDFC Securities):
- Equity delivery: 0.30%-0.55% of transaction
- Equity intraday: 0.05%-0.10%
- F&O (futures, options): ₹20-₹100 per lot (or as % of premium)
- Currency: ₹20-₹50 per lot

Discount brokers (Zerodha, Groww, Upstox, 5paisa, Angel One):
- Equity delivery: Free (₹0) at most discount brokers
- Equity intraday: ₹20 or 0.03% (whichever lower) per executed order
- F&O: Flat ₹20 per executed order
- Currency: ₹20 per order or per lot

Additional Charges (across brokers):
- STT (Securities Transaction Tax): 0.10% on equity delivery (each side), 0.025% on intraday sell, 0.10% on options sell of premium, etc.
- SEBI turnover charges: ₹10 per crore
- Exchange transaction charge: 0.00322% on NSE equity (approx)
- Stamp duty: 0.015% on delivery buy, 0.003% on intraday/F&O buy
- GST 18% on brokerage + transaction + SEBI charges

These are statutory and largely uniform across brokers.

IPO APPLICATION VIA ASBA
ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) is the SEBI-mandated way to apply for IPOs:
- Investor's funds blocked in own bank account (not transferred)
- If allotted: Funds debited
- If not allotted (or partial): Block released
- Apply via:
  - Net banking IPO section
  - UPI mandate (retail investors up to ₹5 lakh)
  - Mobile app
- All major banks offer ASBA
- No charges to investor

Lot size: 1 lot minimum (typically 8-30 shares for retail at ₹15,000 lot value)
Maximum retail bid: ₹2 lakh
Bidding window: Usually 3-5 days for mainboard IPOs

POST-LISTING:
- Shares credited to demat account
- Listing day price discovery
- Can sell or hold

NPS — NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM
Long-term retirement scheme regulated by PFRDA:
- Tier I: Lock-in till age 60; mandatory annuity at exit
- Tier II: Voluntary, no lock-in (like mutual fund)
- Minimum contribution Tier I: ₹500 / month or ₹6,000 / year
- Asset allocation choice: Auto (age-based) or Active (Equity ≤ 75%)
- Returns: 9%-11% historically (market-linked)
- Tax benefits:
  - Section 80C: Up to ₹1.5 lakh on contribution
  - Section 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 exclusive (over and above 80C)
  - Section 80CCD(2): Employer's contribution up to 10% of basic (no limit for old tax regime, 14% for some govt employees)
- On exit:
  - 60% of corpus tax-free withdrawal
  - 40% must purchase annuity (taxable)

NPS opened via:
- eNPS portal
- NSDL CRA
- Banks (POP — Point of Presence): SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, others
- Standalone agents (POP-SP)

SOVEREIGN GOLD BONDS (SGB)
Issued by RBI on behalf of Government of India:
- Tenure: 8 years (early redemption from 5th year)
- Interest: 2.5% p.a. paid semi-annually (in addition to gold price appreciation)
- Capital gains tax-free if held till maturity
- No making charges or storage worry vs. physical gold
- Apply via banks, post offices, stock exchanges
- ISIN-mapped; held in demat or paper form
- Loan against SGB possible

(Note: Government has paused new SGB issuance from FY24-25; check current status. Existing SGBs continue to be tradable.)

RETAIL DIRECT — RBI's DIRECT G-SEC INVESTMENT PORTAL
- Retail investors can buy government securities (T-bills, dated bonds, SDLs) directly via RBI's Retail Direct portal
- Free account opening
- Bid in auctions or buy from secondary market
- Direct investment without intermediary
- rbiretaildirect.org.in

TAXATION OF INVESTMENTS

Equity / Equity Mutual Funds:
- STCG (held < 12 months): 20% (raised from 15% in Budget 2024)
- LTCG (held > 12 months): 12.5% on gains exceeding ₹1.25 lakh per year (revised in Budget 2024)
- Dividend: Taxable at slab rate; TDS 10% if ₹5,000+ in a year

Debt Mutual Funds (purchased on or after 1 April 2023):
- Always taxed at slab rate, regardless of holding period (indexation benefit removed)

Stocks (direct holdings): Same as equity MF.

Hybrid funds: Depending on equity allocation:
- 65%+ equity: Treated as equity
- < 65% equity: Treated as debt

Bonds / Debentures:
- Listed debt: STCG (< 12 months) slab rate; LTCG (> 12 months) 12.5%
- Interest: Slab rate

Gold ETF / Gold MF:
- STCG (< 24 months): Slab rate
- LTCG (> 24 months): 12.5%

FD / RD / Savings Interest: Slab rate (TDS applicable)

CAPITAL GAINS HARVESTING
Strategy to use the ₹1.25 lakh LTCG exemption annually:
- Sell equity holdings at year-end with gains ≤ ₹1.25 lakh, buy back next day
- Resets the cost base higher
- Used judiciously, can save significant tax

LOAN AGAINST SECURITIES
- Pledge demat holdings or mutual funds
- Get loan at 9%-12% p.a.
- LTV: 50% for equity, 75% for debt
- Useful for short-term liquidity without selling
- Banks: SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Bajaj Finance offer

INVESTMENT PRODUCTS DISTRIBUTED VIA BANKS
1. Mutual funds (Regular Plan distribution)
2. NPS
3. PPF (Public Provident Fund) — direct, no distributor commission
4. Tax-saver schemes
5. SGB (Sovereign Gold Bond)
6. SSY (Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana)
7. SCSS (Senior Citizen Savings Scheme)
8. Floating Rate Savings Bonds (RBI bonds, 7-year)
9. RBI Retail Direct (for G-Sec)
10. Insurance + investment products (ULIP, endowment) — often mis-sold

WORDS OF CAUTION ON BANK DISTRIBUTION
- Banks earn commission on mutual fund / insurance / ULIP sales
- Aggressive cross-selling can lead to unsuitable products
- "Right to Suitability" — refuse if not appropriate
- Direct plans always cheaper than regular
- Verify products and read documents before signing
- "Don't sign blank forms" — always fill before signing

INVESTMENT TIPS FOR BANK CUSTOMERS

1. Start with PPF and ELSS for tax saving
2. Add SIP in index funds for long-term equity (Nifty 50 / Sensex)
3. Maintain emergency fund in liquid fund or savings account (3-6 months expenses)
4. Diversify across asset classes (equity, debt, gold, real estate)
5. Avoid insurance + investment combo products (ULIP, endowment) — buy term insurance + invest separately
6. Stay invested long-term; avoid trying to time the market
7. Rebalance annually
8. Maximise EPF / NPS for retirement
9. Use SWP for retirement income
10. Track via consolidated accounts (CAMS Consolidated Account Statement)

Rates and rules may change, verify with bank / AMC / SEBI / PFRDA. Mutual funds and securities investments are subject to market risk — read all scheme-related documents carefully.
