GOVERNMENT SAVINGS SCHEMES — PPF, NSC, SSY, SCSS, KVP, MIS, FRSB

OVERVIEW
The Government of India offers a basket of small savings schemes that provide assured returns backed by sovereign guarantee. These schemes are distributed through banks (most schemes) and post offices (all schemes), and feature attractive interest rates that are revised quarterly by the Ministry of Finance. They are particularly suitable for risk-averse investors, retirees, and tax planning. All rates below are indicative as of FY 2024-25; rates may change quarterly, verify with bank/post office.

PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND (PPF)

Tenure: 15 years (extendable in 5-year blocks indefinitely)

Interest Rate: 7.1% p.a. (compounded annually, credited at year-end). Government revises quarterly; has been 7.1% for several quarters as of FY24-25.

Investment Limits:
- Minimum: ₹500 per year
- Maximum: ₹1,50,000 per year (combined across all PPF accounts of an individual)
- Investment can be in lump sum or up to 12 installments

Eligibility:
- Any resident Indian individual (one account per person)
- Minor account allowed; combined limit applies with parent
- NRIs cannot open new PPF; existing PPF before becoming NRI can continue till maturity
- HUF cannot open PPF

Tax Benefits (Triple Exempt — EEE):
- Contribution: Deductible under Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh
- Interest: Fully tax-free
- Maturity: Fully tax-free
- One of the very few EEE instruments available

Account Opening:
- Banks: SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, Canara, PNB, IOB and many others
- Post offices nationwide
- Online opening via net banking (for existing customers in some banks)

Loan Facility:
- Available from 3rd to 6th year of opening
- Up to 25% of balance at end of 2nd year preceding loan year
- Interest 1% above prevailing PPF rate
- Repayable in 36 months

Partial Withdrawal:
- From 7th financial year onwards
- Up to 50% of balance at end of 4th year preceding or current year, whichever is lower
- Once per financial year

Premature Closure:
- Allowed after 5 years (only for specific reasons: serious illness, higher education, change of residency)
- Interest penalty: 1% off the applicable PPF rate

Extension after 15 years:
- Option 1: Continue without further contribution; balance continues to earn interest
- Option 2: Extend with continued contribution in blocks of 5 years (notify within 1 year of maturity)

PPF is widely regarded as the cornerstone of long-term Indian savings — safe, tax-efficient, and stable.

NATIONAL SAVINGS CERTIFICATE (NSC)

Tenure: 5 years (fixed; no premature withdrawal except special circumstances)

Interest Rate: 7.7% p.a. (compounded annually but paid out only at maturity).

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹1,000 and multiples of ₹100
- Maximum: No upper limit
- Standalone or jointly held

Tax Benefits:
- Contribution: Deductible under Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh
- Interest earned each year (except final year) is deemed reinvested and qualifies for 80C
- Final year interest: Taxable as "Income from Other Sources"
- TDS: Not deducted; tax paid by investor

Maturity: ₹100 invested today matures to approximately ₹144.84 at 7.7% over 5 years.

Available at: All post offices, select banks (limited).

Use Cases:
- Tax-saving with assured return
- Collateral for loans (can be pledged)

Note: Tax-saver bank FDs and ELSS are alternative 80C instruments with similar/better features (ELSS has higher upside but market-linked risk).

SUKANYA SAMRIDDHI YOJANA (SSY)

For: Girl child up to 10 years of age

Tenure: 21 years from account opening, or 18 years of girl, whichever earlier; contribution until 15 years from opening

Interest Rate: 8.2% p.a. (compounded annually) — among the highest small-savings rates

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹250 per year
- Maximum: ₹1,50,000 per year (combined across all SSY accounts)

Maximum Accounts:
- Two girl child per family (third if twins/triplets at second birth)
- One account per girl

Account Opening:
- Banks (SBI, ICICI, HDFC, Axis, BoB, PNB, others)
- Post offices
- Documents: Girl child's birth certificate, parent's KYC

Tax Benefits (EEE):
- Contribution: Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh
- Interest: Tax-free
- Maturity: Tax-free
- Best tax-saving option for girl child education / marriage

Withdrawal:
- 50% partial withdrawal allowed at age 18 for higher education
- Full closure at marriage (after 18 years)
- Maturity at 21 years from opening

SSY is one of the most attractive long-term schemes due to 8.2% tax-free returns. Strongly recommended for families with a girl child.

SENIOR CITIZEN SAVINGS SCHEME (SCSS)

For: Indian residents aged 60+ (also 55-60 if retired with retirement benefits)

Tenure: 5 years (extendable in 3-year blocks once)

Interest Rate: 8.2% p.a. paid quarterly (one of the highest secured rates for seniors)

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹1,000
- Maximum: ₹30,00,000 (raised from ₹15 lakh in Budget 2023; per individual, not combined for spouse)
- One-time lump sum deposit

Account Opening:
- All major banks and post offices

Tax Benefits:
- Contribution: Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh
- Interest: Fully taxable; TDS if interest > ₹50,000 (combined with savings/FD for senior under 80TTB; ₹50,000 deduction)

Premature Withdrawal:
- Allowed after 1 year (with penalty)
- 1-2 years: 1.5% penalty
- 2-5 years: 1% penalty

Quarterly Payout:
- Interest credited every quarter (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct)
- Provides regular income stream for retirees

SCSS is the most popular retirement instrument for tax-paying seniors, offering 8.2% assured quarterly income.

KISAN VIKAS PATRA (KVP)

For: Any Indian individual or trust

Tenure: 115 months (currently — adjusted by govt periodically based on rate); KVP doubles money

Interest Rate: 7.5% p.a. (compounded annually) — implied yield such that ₹100 becomes ₹200 over 115 months

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹1,000
- Maximum: No upper limit
- Available in denominations of ₹1,000, ₹5,000, ₹10,000, ₹50,000

Tax: Interest is taxable; no 80C benefit on contribution; no TDS

Maturity: Lump sum payout

Premature Encashment: Allowed after 2.5 years with reduced rate

Available at: Post offices; some banks

KVP is suited for those wanting an assured-doubling instrument without tax consideration.

POST OFFICE MONTHLY INCOME SCHEME (POMIS)

Tenure: 5 years

Interest Rate: 7.4% p.a. paid monthly

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹1,500
- Maximum: ₹9,00,000 single account; ₹15,00,000 joint account (raised from ₹4.5 lakh and ₹9 lakh in Budget 2023)

Tax: Interest taxable; no 80C benefit

Available at: All post offices (banks don't offer this)

POMIS is similar to SCSS in nature (monthly income) but with lower interest and lower cap, available to any individual (not just seniors).

POST OFFICE TIME DEPOSIT

Tenure: 1, 2, 3, or 5 years

Interest Rate:
- 1 year: 6.9% p.a.
- 2 year: 7.0%
- 3 year: 7.1%
- 5 year: 7.5%

Investment: Minimum ₹1,000; no upper limit

Tax: 5-year TD eligible for Section 80C deduction; interest taxable

Available at: Post offices

POST OFFICE RECURRING DEPOSIT

Tenure: 5 years (extendable in 5-year blocks)

Interest Rate: 6.7% p.a. (compounded quarterly)

Investment: Minimum ₹100 per month

Tax: No 80C; interest taxable

FLOATING RATE SAVINGS BONDS (FRSB) — RBI BONDS

Tenure: 7 years (lock-in)

Interest Rate: Floating, linked to NSC rate + 35 basis points; revised half-yearly. Currently ~8.05% p.a. paid semi-annually (Jan and Jul).

Investment:
- Minimum: ₹1,000 (in multiples)
- Maximum: No upper limit

Tax: Fully taxable (slab rate); TDS applicable if interest > ₹10,000

Lock-in:
- 7 years for general investors
- Reduced lock-in for senior citizens (4-6 years depending on age):
  - 60-70 years: 6 years
  - 70-80 years: 5 years
  - 80+ years: 4 years

Available at: All major banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, etc.) and RBI's Retail Direct portal

FRSB is attractive for those wanting high interest with sovereign guarantee and accepting the longer lock-in. Senior citizens benefit from reduced lock-in.

PRADHAN MANTRI VAYA VANDANA YOJANA (PMVVY)
Pension scheme for seniors offered via LIC. Now closed for fresh enrollment from March 2023. Existing policies continue.

MAHILA SAMMAN SAVINGS CERTIFICATE
Introduced in Budget 2023:
- For women / girls only
- Tenure: 2 years (one-time scheme; subscription window 2023-2025)
- Interest: 7.5% p.a. (compounded quarterly)
- Investment: ₹1,000 minimum, ₹2,00,000 maximum
- Partial withdrawal allowed
- Available at post offices

ATAL PENSION YOJANA (APY)
For unorganised sector / informal workers aged 18-40 years:
- Guaranteed pension of ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 per month after age 60
- Contribution varies based on entry age and chosen pension
- Government co-contribution for first 5 years for eligible subscribers
- Tax benefit under Section 80CCD
- Open via banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, others) and post offices

PRADHAN MANTRI JEEVAN JYOTI BIMA YOJANA (PMJJBY) and PRADHAN MANTRI SURAKSHA BIMA YOJANA (PMSBY)
Government's flagship low-cost insurance schemes:
- PMJJBY: ₹2 lakh life insurance for ₹436/year, age 18-50, renewable till 55
- PMSBY: ₹2 lakh accident insurance for ₹20/year, age 18-70
- Auto-debit from bank account
- Major banks distribute these

NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM (NPS)
- See investment document
- Retirement-focused; market-linked
- Tax benefit u/s 80CCD(1B) — ₹50,000 exclusive

QUARTERLY RATE REVISION
Small savings rates are reviewed every quarter by the Ministry of Finance:
- Notification published end of each quarter for the next quarter
- Based on G-sec yields + spread
- Locked-in deposits earn at the rate prevailing at the time of investment (subsequent quarters affect new deposits only)
- PPF and SSY rates apply for the year (or per government's interpretation)

WHERE TO OPEN/INVEST

Banks (most schemes):
- SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, PNB, Canara, IOB, etc.
- Net banking integration for some schemes (PPF, SSY)
- Branch visit for SCSS, FRSB

Post Offices (all schemes):
- Especially for KVP, POMIS, POTD, SCSS, RD, NSC
- India Post Payments Bank (IPPB) — digital account complement

Online aggregators / advisors:
- ETMoney, Groww, Kuvera for tracking (not direct investment in govt schemes)

TIPS

1. Max out PPF (₹1.5 lakh/year) for tax-free long-term wealth
2. Open SSY for girl child immediately on birth — 21 years of compounding at 8.2% tax-free is huge
3. Use SCSS + POMIS for retirement (₹39 lakh combined — ₹30 lakh SCSS + ₹9 lakh POMIS single)
4. NSC / 5-yr tax-saver FD for 80C if not using ELSS
5. FRSB for retirement-stage income, especially seniors with reduced lock-in
6. Diversify between government schemes (safety) and equity MF (growth)
7. Set up automated deductions from savings account (PPF auto-debit)
8. Track maturity dates and renew/reinvest promptly

Rates may change, verify with bank/post office for current quarterly rates and detailed scheme features.
