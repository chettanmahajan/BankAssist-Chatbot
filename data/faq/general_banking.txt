GENERAL BANKING FAQ — HOLIDAYS, HOURS, CUSTOMER ID, NOMINEE, LOCKER

BANK HOLIDAYS IN INDIA
Bank holidays in India are governed by the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 and notified by each state government for its territory. They typically include:
- All Sundays
- 2nd and 4th Saturdays of every month (working: 1st, 3rd, 5th Saturdays — half day in some banks)
- Public holidays under the Negotiable Instruments Act (NI Act)
- State-specific holidays declared by respective State Governments

Common all-India bank holidays:
- Republic Day (26 January)
- Independence Day (15 August)
- Gandhi Jayanti (2 October)
- Holi (variable date, March)
- Eid (variable, twice a year)
- Christmas (25 December)
- Diwali / Deepavali (variable, October-November)
- Good Friday (variable, March-April)
- April 1: Annual closing of accounts (banks closed for public)
- Other regional holidays: Pongal (TN), Onam (Kerala), Bihu (Assam), Durga Puja (WB), Eid Milad-un-Nabi, Buddha Purnima, Mahavir Jayanti, Guru Nanak Jayanti

Note: RTGS and NEFT now operate 24×7 including holidays (since 2020). UPI is always available. Branch services are not available on holidays.

BANK WORKING HOURS
Standard working hours (most banks):
- Monday to Friday: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM (some branches extend to 5:00 PM)
- Saturday (1st, 3rd, 5th): 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM (half day)
- Saturday (2nd, 4th): Closed
- Sunday: Closed
- Lunch break: Some branches close briefly 2:00 PM-2:30 PM

Cash transaction hours:
- Cash counter typically open 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM or 3:00 PM
- After cash hours, only basic services (statement, account inquiry, locker)

Extended-hour branches:
- Some private bank branches operate till 7:00 PM (HDFC, ICICI in select metros)
- Airport / hospital branches: 24×7 or extended hours
- ATMs / Self-service kiosks: 24×7

Drop boxes (cheques): Available at branch entrance for 24×7 deposit (less common now due to security concerns).

CUSTOMER ID (CIF — CUSTOMER INFORMATION FILE)
- A unique number assigned to each customer by the bank
- Different from account number; one customer may have multiple accounts (savings, current, FD, loan) all linked to same CIF
- 9 to 12 digits typically
- Where to find it:
  - Top of welcome kit
  - Net banking login page (often shown as User ID or near it)
  - Bank statement header
  - Passbook first page
  - SMS from bank
  - Branch enquiry with KYC
- Used for: Linking accounts, opening additional products, internal bank operations

ACCOUNT NUMBER, IFSC, MICR
- Account number: 9-18 digits, unique to each account
- IFSC (Indian Financial System Code): 11-character alpha-numeric code identifying bank + branch (e.g., HDFC0001234)
- MICR (Magnetic Ink Character Recognition): 9-digit code printed at bottom of cheque (city + bank + branch)

NOMINATION — KEY FACTS

Why nominate:
- Nomination ensures bank funds are paid to the nominee on death of account holder, without going through legal succession (will, succession certificate)
- Saves family from prolonged paperwork during emotional time
- Strongly recommended for all account types

When to nominate:
- At account opening (Form DA-1 attached with AOF)
- Anytime later (submit Form DA-1 at branch or via app/net banking)
- For each product separately (savings, FD, RD, locker, demat) — though some banks allow bundled nomination

Nominee details:
- Only 1 nominee per account / FD / RD per RBI norms
- Nominee can be any individual: spouse, child, sibling, parent, friend
- Minor can be nominated (with guardian appointed to receive on minor's behalf)
- Nominee details: Full name, address, relationship, age, signature

Nomination doesn't override legal heirship:
- Nominee receives funds as trustee for legal heirs
- Legal heirs (as per will or Hindu Succession Act / Indian Succession Act) have ultimate ownership
- For peace of mind: Make a Will alongside nomination

Cancelling / changing nomination:
- Form DA-2 (Cancellation) or fresh DA-1 (Change)
- Free of cost
- Joint nomination not permitted

Joint account nomination:
- Operative on death of all joint holders
- Each joint holder can have separate nominations for their share (subject to bank's policy)

NOMINEE CLAIM PROCESS
On death of account holder:
1. Inform bank with death certificate (original + copies)
2. Submit Claim Form (provided by bank)
3. Nominee's ID and address proof
4. Photograph of nominee
5. Indemnity bond (in some cases for amounts above thresholds)
6. Bank verifies and pays out within 15 days typically (per RBI guidelines)

For accounts without nomination:
- Legal heirs approach bank with:
  - Death certificate
  - Letter of Administration / Succession Certificate / Will probate
  - Indemnity bond
  - Affidavit from heirs
- Process takes 30-90 days
- For amounts below ₹1 lakh (some banks ₹5 lakh), simpler procedure: indemnity bond + affidavit without succession certificate

LOCKERS (SAFE DEPOSIT VAULTS)
A safe deposit locker is a physical secure box in the bank's strong room rented to customers for storing valuables — jewellery, documents, gold, silver, important papers.

Eligibility:
- Any individual with a savings/current account at the bank
- Joint locker: Up to 2-4 holders typically; operation mode (E/S, J/S) similar to joint account

Sizes and rental:
- Small (3" × 5" × 22"): ₹1,000-₹2,000 per year
- Medium (5" × 5" × 22"): ₹2,500-₹4,500
- Large (10" × 5" × 22"): ₹5,000-₹8,000
- Extra-large: ₹10,000-₹15,000
- Metro rents are higher

Security deposit / linked FD:
- One-time FD requirement: ₹10,000-₹50,000 depending on locker size
- Earns interest at FD rate
- Refundable on locker surrender

Locker operations:
- Visit branch during working hours
- Sign locker register with date and time
- Branch officer accompanies to vault, opens master lock
- Customer opens their lock (key + access card)
- Both locks must engage for locker to be opened
- Branch officer steps away while customer accesses contents (privacy)
- Free visits: 10-12 per year typically; extra ₹50-₹150 per visit

What's NOT allowed in locker:
- Cash (some banks technically allow; not advisable as not insured separately)
- Arms, ammunition, explosives
- Narcotics
- Anything illegal or perishable

Locker insurance:
- Banks are not liable for contents loss due to acts of God, war, riots, terrorism (per locker agreement)
- Bank IS liable for negligence, fraud, fire (with limits — typically 100x annual rent per RBI guidelines)
- Consider separate locker contents insurance for high-value items

Loss of locker key:
- Pay for key replacement (₹250-₹2,500)
- Locker may need to be drilled if key lost — additional ₹2,000-₹5,000
- Customer must be present during drilling

Locker surrender:
- Return all keys
- Empty the locker
- Surrender access card
- FD released back to customer

LOCKER WAITING LIST
Due to limited locker inventory, most metro branches have a waiting list (some 1-3 years). New branches may have immediate availability.

DEBIT FREEZE / ACCOUNT FREEZE
Reasons for freeze:
1. Court order (creditor suit, marital dispute, income tax)
2. Income Tax notice (Section 226(3))
3. Suspected fraud / suspicious transaction
4. KYC pending / lapsed
5. Customer request (lost cheque book, anti-fraud control)

Effects:
- No debits allowed
- Credits may continue
- Transactions returned with "Account Frozen"

Reactivation:
- Submit relevant documents (court vacation order, KYC update, etc.)
- Branch processes within 1-7 days

DORMANT / INOPERATIVE ACCOUNT
- Account marked "Inoperative" after 12 months of no customer-initiated transaction
- "Dormant" after 24 months
- Reactivation: Visit branch with KYC + small deposit/withdrawal request
- Auto-credit (interest, EMI receipt, salary) doesn't reset the clock; only customer-initiated transactions do
- After 10 years inoperative, funds transferred to RBI's Depositor Education and Awareness Fund (DEAF) — reclaimable anytime via UDGAM portal (udgam.rbi.org.in)

LOST / STOLEN ITEMS — PROCESS
1. Cheque book lost: Block via app / customer care; submit FIR copy for new book
2. Debit card lost: Block immediately; replacement in 7-10 days
3. ATM card captured: Visit branch with ID; bank retrieves and reissues
4. Passbook lost: Apply for duplicate at branch with ₹100-₹300 fee
5. Account lost (don't know account number): Visit branch with KYC; staff retrieves from CIF

ADDRESS / CONTACT UPDATE
Update via:
- Net banking / mobile app: Upload new address proof, OTP-verified
- Branch: Submit Letter of Request + new address proof
- FREE of charge typically

Affects:
- Account communication
- Cheque book delivery
- KYC compliance (re-KYC eligibility)
- Insurance / investment-linked products

MOBILE NUMBER / EMAIL UPDATE
Crucial for security (OTPs, alerts):
- Visit branch with KYC and account details
- Online: Net banking → Update Contact → OTP to old number for verification
- Some banks: Update via ATM (PIN + new number)

ADD JOINT HOLDER
Add a joint holder to existing account:
- Both holders visit branch
- KYC of new holder
- Fresh signature card
- Bank processes; usually doesn't require new account number

REMOVE JOINT HOLDER
- Trickier — depends on operation mode and bank policy
- Generally requires all holders' consent
- May involve closing and re-opening the account
- Outstanding instruments must be cleared first

TRANSFER ACCOUNT TO ANOTHER BRANCH
- Useful when changing city/job
- Submit Transfer Request Form at home branch or new branch
- Account number stays the same (same bank); just branch IFSC/MICR changes
- Process: 7-15 days

CIBIL / CREDIT SCORE
- 3-digit score (300-900) generated by CIBIL (TransUnion)
- 750+ is excellent; 700-749 good; 650-699 fair; below 650 poor
- Maintain by:
  - Paying credit card bills in full and on time
  - Paying EMIs on time
  - Limiting hard credit inquiries
  - Keeping credit utilisation below 30%
  - Maintaining old credit cards (history length helps)
- Free annual report from each bureau (CIBIL, Experian, CRIF, Equifax) via their websites

ESCALATION FRAMEWORK
If service issues:
1. Branch / customer care (TAT 7-30 days)
2. Internal Ombudsman (large banks)
3. Banking Ombudsman via cms.rbi.org.in (within 1 year of bank's response)
4. Consumer Court for specific damages

RIGHTS UNDER RBI CHARTER OF CUSTOMER RIGHTS
- Right to Fair Treatment
- Right to Transparency, Fair and Honest Dealing
- Right to Suitability (product matching customer profile)
- Right to Privacy
- Right to Grievance Redress and Compensation

For specific bank policies, processes, and current rates/charges, refer to the bank's website or visit the branch. Rates and rules may change, verify with bank.
