GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL — STEP-BY-STEP ESCALATION GUIDE

OVERVIEW
Every bank customer has the right to fair grievance redress. India has a well-defined, multi-level grievance redressal mechanism, regulated by RBI, that ensures customer complaints are heard, investigated, and resolved within reasonable timeframes. Knowing this process empowers customers to seek timely resolution and, where applicable, monetary compensation.

THE GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL HIERARCHY

Level 1: Branch / Customer Care
Level 2: Internal Ombudsman (for large banks)
Level 3: RBI Banking Ombudsman (Integrated Ombudsman Scheme)
Level 4: Appellate Authority (Deputy Governor, RBI)
Level 5: Consumer Court / Civil Court (parallel route)

LEVEL 1 — APPROACH THE BANK FIRST (MANDATORY)
All grievances must first be submitted to the bank itself. Channels:

A. Branch:
- Visit branch manager or designated grievance officer
- Submit complaint in writing
- Get acknowledgment with reference number
- Branches typically have a Grievance Register

B. Customer Care:
- Toll-free helpline (24×7 for most banks)
- IVR option for grievance / complaint
- Note SR (Service Request) number generated

C. Email:
- Bank's grievance email (e.g., customercare@bankname.com, grievance.officer@bankname.com)
- Keep written record

D. Net Banking / Mobile App:
- Most banks have "Raise Complaint" / "Grievance" section in app/portal
- Categorise the issue, attach documents
- Track status

E. Bank's website:
- Online complaint form
- "Contact Us" → "File a Complaint"
- Some banks have a structured complaint portal (e.g., HDFC Grievance, ICICI Customer Care Portal)

What to include in your complaint:
1. Account number / customer ID
2. Date of incident
3. Detailed description (sequence of events)
4. Amount involved (if applicable)
5. Supporting documents (statements, screenshots, copies of communication)
6. Specific resolution sought
7. Your contact details

TURNAROUND TIME (TAT) AT LEVEL 1
RBI mandates banks to resolve customer complaints within 30 days. Most banks have internal TATs:
- Acknowledgment: Within 1-3 working days
- First response with status update: 5-7 working days
- Final resolution: 21-30 days
- Complex disputes (chargebacks, fraud): 90 days

If the bank does not resolve or the customer is unsatisfied with the resolution, proceed to next level.

LEVEL 2 — INTERNAL OMBUDSMAN (IO)
RBI mandated Internal Ombudsman for all banks with deposits above a certain threshold (currently ≥ ₹50,000 crore) since 2018. IO is independent of business operations and reports to bank's Audit Committee.

Trigger: When the bank's first-line grievance resolution is rejected or only partially satisfactory.

Process:
- Bank's grievance handling team auto-escalates rejected / partial complaints to IO
- Customer doesn't need to apply separately for IO; it's auto-triggered
- IO independently reviews the complaint
- Issues decision; if upheld in customer's favour, bank must comply
- IO resolution typically within 30 days

LEVEL 3 — RBI BANKING OMBUDSMAN (INTEGRATED SCHEME 2021)
RBI's external grievance redressal mechanism. The Reserve Bank — Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021 unified three earlier schemes (Banking Ombudsman, NBFC Ombudsman, Digital Payments Ombudsman) into ONE scheme covering:
- Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Scheduled Primary (Urban) Cooperative Banks with deposits ≥ ₹50 crore
- NBFCs (deposit-taking + non-deposit-taking with assets ≥ ₹100 crore)
- Non-bank Pre-paid Payment Instruments (PPIs) issuers

When to file:
- The bank has rejected your complaint, OR
- You haven't received a reply within 30 days of filing complaint, OR
- The reply received is not satisfactory

Time limit:
- Within 1 year from the date of bank's reply OR from when 30 days lapse without reply, whichever earlier
- Some matters: Within 1 year from date the cause of action arose

Filing channels:
1. Online: cms.rbi.org.in (Complaint Management System portal)
2. Toll-free: 14448 (8 AM-8 PM, IST)
3. Email: CRPC@rbi.org.in (Centralised Receipt and Processing Centre, Chandigarh)
4. Physical: Post to "Centralised Receipt and Processing Centre, Reserve Bank of India, 4th Floor, Sector 17, Chandigarh - 160017"

What to submit:
- Personal details (name, address, mobile, email)
- Bank's name, branch, account number
- Complaint nature (one of the prescribed categories — non-payment, deficient service, fraudulent transaction, etc.)
- Date of bank's reply (or evidence of 30-day non-reply)
- Loss / relief sought
- Supporting documents

Ombudsman process:
1. Receipt: Complaint registered, acknowledgment with reference number
2. Examination: Initial screening for completeness and scope
3. Conciliation: Ombudsman tries to facilitate settlement between customer and bank
4. Award: If conciliation fails, Ombudsman issues a formal decision
5. Acceptance: Customer can accept (binding) or reject the award

Outcome:
- Award up to ₹20 lakh for direct loss
- Additional up to ₹1 lakh for compensation for mental agony, time lost
- If customer accepts: Bank must comply within 30 days
- If bank doesn't comply: RBI may take regulatory action

Free of cost: No fees / lawyer required.

Out-of-scope:
- Matters under court / tribunal
- Matters where 1-year filing window has expired
- Vexatious / frivolous complaints

LEVEL 4 — APPELLATE AUTHORITY (RBI DEPUTY GOVERNOR)
If the Ombudsman's award doesn't satisfy either the customer or the bank, an appeal can be filed:
- By customer: If complaint rejected by Ombudsman, can appeal
- By bank: With bank's CEO approval, within 30 days of receiving award
- Filed with Appellate Authority — Deputy Governor in-charge of RBI's Consumer Education and Protection Department
- Appellate decision is final (subject to writ jurisdiction of High Court)

LEVEL 5 — CONSUMER COURT / CIVIL COURT
Parallel to Ombudsman process, customer can directly approach:

Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (under Consumer Protection Act, 2019):
- District Commission: Claims up to ₹1 crore
- State Commission: Claims ₹1 crore-₹10 crore
- National Commission: Above ₹10 crore

Filing:
- File complaint with the Commission having territorial jurisdiction
- Pay nominal fee (₹100-₹5,000 based on claim value)
- Can engage lawyer or self-represent
- Resolution timeframe: 90 days to 1 year typically
- Award: Refund, compensation, punitive damages, costs

Civil Court:
- For larger / complex disputes outside consumer law
- Higher costs and longer timelines
- Generally used as last resort

Note: Customer can pursue Banking Ombudsman OR Consumer Court — not both simultaneously for the same matter. Choose based on:
- Nature of issue (consumer rights → Consumer Court; banking-specific → Ombudsman)
- Speed (Ombudsman generally faster, free)
- Value of claim
- Need for compensation beyond ₹20 lakh

SPECIFIC COMPLAINT CATEGORIES AND TIMELINES

A. Failed ATM Cash Withdrawal:
- Auto-reversal: Within T+5 working days (after that, ₹100/day compensation)
- Customer report: Within 30 days of transaction
- Bank investigation: Within 7 working days

B. Failed UPI / IMPS / Online Transfer:
- Auto-reversal: Within T+1 working day usually
- If pending beyond 24 hours: Raise dispute via UPI app or net banking
- UDIR (UPI Dispute and Issue Resolution) framework: T+3 day resolution

C. Unauthorised Electronic Transaction:
- Report within 3 working days for ZERO liability
- 4-7 days: Limited liability (₹10k-₹25k per RBI cap)
- Bank investigation: 90 days max

D. Loan Disputes (interest calculation, foreclosure, etc.):
- Branch / loan service centre: 30 days
- Escalate to Nodal Officer
- Banking Ombudsman: After Bank's response or 30 days lapse

E. Deficient Service (passbook, statement, KYC pending):
- Branch / customer care: 7-15 days
- Escalate to Nodal Officer: 30 days
- Banking Ombudsman

F. Mis-sold Insurance / Investment:
- Insurance grievance via IRDAI's Bima Bharosa portal
- Mutual fund grievance via SCORES (SEBI Complaint Redress System)
- Banking Ombudsman for banking-related mis-sale

CONSEQUENCES FOR BANKS — REGULATORY ENFORCEMENT
RBI penalises banks for systemic failures:
- ₹1 lakh-₹10 crore fines for KYC lapses
- Cease-and-desist orders for unfair practices
- Customer service penalties
- Reputational damage from publicly disclosed penalty orders

COMPENSATION FRAMEWORK — EXAMPLES

1. Failed ATM auto-reversal delay: ₹100/day after T+5
2. Unauthorised electronic transaction (zero liability): Full refund + reversal of interest/fees + ₹100/day delayed credit penalty
3. Failed cheque clearing causing financial loss: Compensation equal to actual loss
4. Late credit of NEFT/RTGS (bank's fault): Penal interest at RBI's repo rate + 2%
5. Foreclosure NOC delay (vehicle loan): ₹100/day after 30 days
6. Branch service deficiency: Symbolic compensation (₹1,000-₹10,000) often awarded by Ombudsman

SCORES, IRDAI, SEBI PORTALS — FOR NON-BANKING GRIEVANCES

For investment / insurance issues:
- SCORES: scores.gov.in (SEBI Complaints Redress System for mutual funds, brokers, depositories)
- IRDAI Bima Bharosa: bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in (insurance)
- IRDAI Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS)
- PFRDA: Pension fund grievances

For service tax / GST related to banking:
- CBIC GST grievance portal

For digital payment specific issues:
- NPCI's UDIR for UPI / IMPS

LEGAL PROVISIONS BACKING GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL

1. Banking Regulation Act, 1949 — RBI's powers over banks
2. RBI Act, 1934 — RBI's regulatory authority
3. Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007 — for payment systems
4. Consumer Protection Act, 2019 — consumer rights
5. Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 — for cheque-related issues
6. Information Technology Act, 2000 — for cyber issues
7. Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 — for data privacy issues

DOCUMENTS TO PRESERVE FOR ANY GRIEVANCE
1. Account opening kit / forms signed
2. Loan / credit card agreement (MITC)
3. Insurance policy documents
4. Bank statements (relevant period)
5. SMS / email alerts of disputed transactions
6. Phone call logs (timestamp / app screenshots)
7. Receipts / acknowledgments from bank
8. Photos / screenshots of evidence
9. Communication trail (emails, letters)

TIPS FOR EFFECTIVE GRIEVANCE FILING
1. State facts chronologically without emotional language
2. Quantify the loss / impact precisely (amount, days delayed, charges incurred)
3. Cite specific RBI circulars / Master Direction provisions
4. Reference the bank's MITC / Schedule of Charges where applicable
5. Attach all documents in PDF (combine into single file if possible)
6. Demand specific resolution: refund, reversal of charges, compensation, written apology
7. Keep professional and polite tone
8. Follow up persistently — note next response date and follow-up
9. Escalate methodically — don't skip levels unless TAT lapses
10. Engage social media (bank's verified Twitter/X handle) for visibility — banks often respond fast to public grievances

PRO-CONSUMER RECENT INITIATIVES
- Sachet portal by RBI (sachet.rbi.org.in) for reporting unauthorised entities like illegal moneylenders, ponzi schemes
- Cybercrime helpline 1930 for online fraud
- DigiSaathi (centralised digital payment helpline)
- ABBA (Annual Banking Awareness — Beti Bachao adverts, customer awareness)

Rates and processes may change, verify with bank / RBI. The fundamental right to fair, prompt grievance redress is enshrined in the RBI Charter and is yours by law.
