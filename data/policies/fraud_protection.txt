FRAUD PROTECTION — REPORTING, ZERO LIABILITY, AND PREVENTION

OVERVIEW
Banking fraud is a serious threat in the digital age. India sees thousands of fraud reports daily — UPI scams, phishing, vishing, card skimming, identity theft, account takeover, fake loan offers, and more. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has built a strong customer protection framework around prompt reporting, limited customer liability, and aggressive investigation. Understanding these provisions and adopting best practices is essential.

RBI'S LIMITED LIABILITY FRAMEWORK FOR UNAUTHORISED ELECTRONIC TRANSACTIONS
RBI Circular: DBR.No.Leg.BC.78/09.07.005/2017-18 dated 6 July 2017.

This circular codifies customer liability for unauthorised electronic banking transactions (including credit/debit card, internet banking, mobile banking, UPI). Three scenarios:

SCENARIO 1 — Contributory fraud, negligence, or deficiency by the bank
Examples:
- Bank's faulty system or data breach
- Bank employee's collusion or negligence
- Inadequate authentication procedures
Customer liability: ZERO, irrespective of whether customer reports or not.

SCENARIO 2 — Third-party breach, neither bank nor customer at fault
Examples:
- Skimming at ATM/POS
- Stolen card details from a merchant data breach
- Fraudulent SIM swap and OTP interception
Customer liability tiered by reporting time:
- Within 3 working days of receiving communication from bank: ZERO liability
- 4-7 working days: Limited per-transaction liability:
  - BSBDA / Salary accounts: ₹5,000
  - Credit card limits up to ₹5 lakh: ₹10,000
  - Other savings accounts: ₹10,000
  - Credit card limits > ₹5 lakh: ₹25,000
- Beyond 7 working days: As per bank's board-approved policy (often higher)

SCENARIO 3 — Customer's own negligence
Examples:
- Sharing PIN / OTP / CVV / password
- Writing PIN on the card
- Falling for phishing / vishing
Customer liability: Bears entire loss UNTIL the unauthorised transaction is reported; after reporting, ZERO further liability.

KEY TAKEAWAY: REPORT FAST. The faster you report (ideally within 3 working days), the more protected you are.

HOW TO REPORT FRAUD — IMMEDIATE STEPS

When you notice an unauthorised transaction (via SMS, email, statement):

Step 1: Block the affected card / freeze the account
- Call bank's 24×7 helpline (printed on back of card)
- Use mobile app's "Block Card" or "Hotlist" feature
- SMS short code (e.g., HDFC: SMS to 73070)
- Net banking → Card Services → Block

Step 2: Report the unauthorised transaction
- Same channel as above
- Submit written email/letter detailing transaction(s) within 3 days
- Get acknowledgment with reference number

Step 3: Bank starts investigation
- Provisional credit may be issued
- Investigation up to 90 days (RBI mandate)
- Resolution communicated to customer

Step 4: File FIR (mandatory for larger amounts)
- Visit cyber police station
- Provide all evidence
- FIR copy strengthens bank's case for chargeback

Step 5: Report to 1930 helpline (National Cybercrime Helpline)
- 24×7 toll-free
- Operated by Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C)
- Quick reporting (within hours) significantly improves recovery chances
- Funds can be frozen in the receiving account if reported fast

Step 6: File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
- Online portal for cyber fraud
- Integrated with police forces nationwide
- Receive case reference for tracking

Step 7: Communicate persistently with bank
- Follow up every few days for resolution
- Escalate to Nodal Officer / Internal Ombudsman if delay
- Banking Ombudsman if not resolved in 30 days from your complaint

TYPES OF BANKING FRAUD

1. PHISHING
Fake email pretending to be from bank/government, asking you to click a link and enter credentials or OTP. Common pretexts: KYC update, account suspended, refund pending, prize won.
Prevention:
- Never click links in unsolicited emails
- Always type bank's URL manually
- Look for HTTPS and the bank's exact domain
- Banks don't ask credentials via email

2. VISHING (VOICE PHISHING)
Fraudster calls posing as bank/RBI/IT department/police, says your card is blocked / suspicious activity / verification pending; asks for OTP, CVV, PIN, or asks you to download a remote-access app.
Prevention:
- Banks and RBI NEVER call asking OTP/PIN/CVV
- Hang up and call back on the official bank number
- Never download AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport on the call's instruction
- RBI repeatedly publishes alerts: "No bank/RBI official asks for OTP"

3. SMISHING (SMS PHISHING)
SMS with fake bank link claiming KYC update, refund, account locked. Often contains shortened URLs.
Prevention:
- Don't click links in SMS
- Forward suspicious SMS to bank's reporting number
- Block sender

4. UPI FRAUD VARIANTS
- Fake "money request" pretending to send money but actually requesting payment
- QR scan to "receive" money — UPI receiving does NOT need PIN; if you're asked to enter PIN, you're SENDING money
- Fake customer care numbers: Google search for bank's number leads to fake; always use the bank's official app/website
- Screen-mirroring apps that show fraudster your phone

5. CARD SKIMMING
- ATM card slot rigged with skimmer device
- Camera captures PIN entry
- Cloned card used for ATM withdrawal abroad (where chip-and-PIN not enforced)
Prevention:
- Inspect card slot for tampering (loose, bulky)
- Cover keypad while entering PIN
- Use bank lobby ATMs (better security)
- Tokenise card details
- Enable transaction alerts

6. CARD TRAPPING
ATM slot mechanically rigged to capture inserted card; fraudster retrieves later.
Prevention:
- Don't accept "help" from strangers
- If card captured, call bank immediately, don't leave it for retrieval

7. CHARGE BACKS AND CARD NOT PRESENT (CNP) FRAUD
Stolen card details used online without OTP (foreign merchants) or via social engineering.
Prevention:
- Tokenisation
- Disable international usage when not travelling
- Set transaction limits
- Use virtual card for risky online merchants

8. ACCOUNT TAKEOVER
Fraudster gains net banking credentials (phishing) and transfers out funds.
Prevention:
- Unique strong password
- Two-factor authentication enforced
- Beneficiary cooling period
- Monitor login alerts

9. SIM SWAP FRAUD
Fraudster obtains duplicate SIM in your name (with KYC of fraudster's accomplice in mobile shop), intercepts OTPs.
Prevention:
- If your SIM suddenly stops working, immediately suspect SIM swap; call mobile operator
- Bank's device-binding requires mobile app on registered phone
- Use UPI PIN (additional layer beyond OTP)

10. JOB OFFER / LOAN APPROVAL FRAUD
Fake job consultant / loan provider asks for "processing fee" upfront. Never delivers.
Prevention:
- Genuine lenders never ask money upfront
- Verify lender on RBI's list of NBFCs/banks
- Avoid Telegram/WhatsApp loan apps from unverified sources

11. INVESTMENT / TRADING FRAUD
- Fake "guaranteed return" schemes
- Bogus crypto / forex platforms
- WhatsApp groups promising trading tips
Prevention:
- SEBI-registered intermediaries only
- "Guaranteed return" is impossible — red flag
- Don't share OTP / KYC docs with anyone

12. FAKE BANK CALLS / FAKE BANK STAFF VISITS
Person visits home claiming to be from bank for "account upgrade", asks for documents/cards.
Prevention:
- Banks don't send unsolicited home visits
- Verify by calling the branch independently

13. CHEQUE FRAUD
- Cheque alteration (amount changed); mitigated by Positive Pay system
- Forged signature; mitigated by signature verification systems
- Cheque kiting; bank software detects patterns

14. KYC EXPIRY / SUSPENSION SCAM
SMS / call claiming KYC pending, account about to be frozen; share Aadhaar OTP to "update".
Prevention:
- KYC updates are done at bank, not via SMS link
- Banks notify well in advance via formal letter / app notification

15. CRYPTOCURRENCY / TRADING APP FRAUD
Apps promising high returns disappear with deposits.
Prevention:
- Only RBI-regulated exchanges (if any) and SEBI-registered platforms
- Verify on official sites

PROTECTIVE TECHNOLOGIES DEPLOYED BY BANKS

1. Two-factor authentication (RBI mandate since 2010 for online card transactions)
2. EMV chip cards (anti-cloning)
3. Tokenisation (effective Oct 2022)
4. Device binding for mobile banking
5. Risk-based AI fraud monitoring (Visa Advanced Authorisation, Mastercard Decision Intelligence)
6. Geo-location validation
7. Velocity checks (e.g., decline if too many transactions in short time)
8. Behavioural biometrics (mobile typing pattern, swipe speed)
9. Beneficiary cooling period (4-24 hours for new payees)
10. Anti-phishing personal image at login
11. Virtual keyboard for sensitive entry
12. SMS / email transaction alerts
13. Card controls in mobile app
14. Daily / per-transaction limits user-configurable

GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES AGAINST CYBER FRAUD

1. National Cybercrime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
- Online reporting of cyber crimes
- Integrated with state police
- Track case status

2. 1930 Cybercrime Helpline
- 24×7 toll-free
- Quick reporting → fund freezing in fraud beneficiary account
- Operated by I4C under MHA

3. Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C)
- Apex body for cyber crime
- 7 verticals: investigation, R&D, training, prevention, awareness, victim support, partnerships

4. Sanchar Saathi Portal
- Block lost mobile, identify mobile connections in your name (against SIM swap)

5. Chakshu Portal
- Report suspicious telecom communications (calls, SMS) for action against fraud numbers
- chakshu.sancharsaathi.gov.in

6. CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team)
- National nodal agency for cyber security
- Publishes vulnerability advisories

PERSONAL SAFETY PROTOCOLS — 30-POINT CHECKLIST

Account & Login:
1. Use unique strong password for net banking (12+ chars, mixed)
2. Change password every 90 days
3. Use biometric login on mobile app
4. Don't save passwords in browser
5. Don't log in on public/shared computers
6. Always log out
7. Don't use public WiFi for banking
8. Update banking app regularly

Transaction:
9. Never share OTP, CVV, PIN, password — banks NEVER ask
10. Verify recipient before sending UPI / NEFT
11. Don't enter UPI PIN to "receive" money — it's only for sending
12. Set conservative daily transaction limits
13. Enable real-time SMS + email alerts
14. Disable international card usage when not abroad
15. Don't use credit card for risky online merchants — use virtual card

Communication:
16. Don't click links in unsolicited SMS/email
17. Don't trust caller ID — fraudsters can spoof
18. Verify by calling bank's official number from bank website
19. Don't install AnyDesk/TeamViewer on call's instruction
20. Don't share screen with anyone over video
21. Don't share account/card photos on social media

Physical:
22. Cover keypad at ATM
23. Inspect ATM card slot for tampering
24. Don't accept help at ATM from strangers
25. Don't write PIN on card
26. Don't leave card unattended at restaurants
27. Shred old bank statements / card slips before disposal

KYC & Identity:
28. Don't share Aadhaar OTP with anyone
29. Mask Aadhaar number on shared copies
30. Monitor your CIBIL report monthly for unfamiliar inquiries

WHAT TO DO IF YOU'VE BEEN DEFRAUDED

Within first hour:
- Call bank's 24×7 helpline; block card/account
- Call 1930 cybercrime helpline
- File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
- File written email to bank's grievance cell

Within 3 days:
- Submit written complaint to bank with all evidence
- File FIR at cyber police station
- Submit complaint at I4C portal
- Inform mobile operator if SIM swap suspected
- Submit chargeback request (for card transactions)

Within 30 days:
- Bank should resolve and credit (zero liability if reported fast)
- If unresolved, escalate to Nodal Officer

Within 1 year:
- File Banking Ombudsman complaint if bank unresponsive
- Consult lawyer for civil suit if loss substantial

PRESERVE EVIDENCE
- SMS / email alerts (forward to your email; don't delete)
- Screen recordings of fraudulent calls if possible
- Bank statements showing transactions
- Communication trail with bank
- Police FIR copy
- Acknowledgment numbers from various authorities

INSURANCE FOR CYBER FRAUD
Some banks bundle cyber fraud insurance with premium debit/credit cards (₹50,000-₹3 lakh coverage). Separate cyber insurance products are also available (₹25 lakh-₹1 crore coverage for individuals; HDFC ERGO, ICICI Lombard, Bajaj Allianz offer these). Premium ₹500-₹5,000 per year.

PSYCHOLOGICAL ASPECT — DON'T PANIC
Fraudsters exploit panic. If you receive a call saying "your account is at risk, transfer to a safe account immediately" — this is fraud. RBI / banks NEVER ask such things. Hang up, take a breath, call back via official number.
