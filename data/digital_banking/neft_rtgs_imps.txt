NEFT, RTGS, AND IMPS — INTER-BANK FUND TRANSFER SYSTEMS

OVERVIEW
India has three major inter-bank fund transfer systems operated by RBI (NEFT, RTGS) and NPCI (IMPS), each serving different transaction profiles. Together with UPI, they cover the spectrum of payment needs: micro-payments to multi-crore corporate transfers. Understanding when to use which is essential for both individuals and businesses.

NEFT — NATIONAL ELECTRONIC FUNDS TRANSFER

What it is: A nation-wide electronic system that facilitates one-to-one fund transfer between any two bank accounts in India. Operated by RBI since 2005.

Settlement model: Deferred Net Settlement (DNS) — transactions are batched and settled at fixed intervals.

Availability:
- Since December 16, 2019: NEFT is available 24×7×365, including Sundays and holidays.
- Earlier was restricted to bank hours and working days only.
- Settlement in half-hourly batches — 48 batches per day.

Transaction limit:
- For retail customers using net/mobile banking: No minimum, no upper limit (set by RBI).
- For walk-in customers at branch without an account: ₹50,000 per transaction.
- Banks may impose their own daily limits for net banking (typically ₹10 lakh-₹50 lakh).

Charges:
- Online (net banking, mobile banking, app): FREE (RBI mandate since January 2020).
- At branch (cash deposit or cheque-based): ₹2.50-₹25 per transaction depending on amount slab.

Processing time:
- Initiation to credit: 30 minutes to 2 hours typically, depending on batch timing
- Each half-hourly batch processes accumulated NEFT requests
- Once credited, beneficiary receives SMS

Information needed to send NEFT:
- Beneficiary name, account number, IFSC code, bank, branch
- Amount and remark
- Beneficiary registration cooling period: 30 minutes to 24 hours per bank policy

Failed transactions:
- Auto-reversal if beneficiary account is wrong or rejected
- Refund usually within same business day; raise dispute if delayed beyond 2 business days

Use cases:
- Personal: Sending money to family, rent, utility payment to non-BBPS
- Business: Vendor payments, salary, refunds
- For non-time-critical transfers where 30-min to 2-hour processing is acceptable

RTGS — REAL-TIME GROSS SETTLEMENT

What it is: RBI's real-time, gross (not netted) settlement system for high-value transfers. "Real-time" means each transaction is settled individually as it comes in, with no delay.

Settlement model: Real-Time Gross Settlement — every transaction is processed individually as it arrives, with immediate credit to beneficiary.

Availability:
- Since December 14, 2020: RTGS is 24×7×365.
- Earlier was limited to working day banking hours.

Transaction limit:
- Minimum: ₹2,00,000 per transaction (RBI mandate — RTGS is exclusively for high-value)
- Maximum: No upper limit (used for corporate large transfers, real estate, securities settlement)

Charges:
- Online: FREE (RBI mandate since 2020).
- At branch:
  - ₹2 lakh to ₹5 lakh: ₹24.50 (₹25 + GST)
  - Above ₹5 lakh: ₹49.50 (₹50 + GST)

Processing time:
- Real-time: Credit to beneficiary's account within 30 minutes (often within seconds) of debit from sender
- Once settled, transaction is irrevocable

Information needed: Same as NEFT — beneficiary name, account, IFSC, bank, branch, amount.

Use cases:
- High-value individual transactions: Property purchase down payment, large investments, family transfer of large sum
- Business: Bulk vendor payments, B2B settlements, real estate, securities, exports/imports settlement
- Anywhere ₹2 lakh+ needs near-instant credit

UNIQUE TRANSACTION REFERENCE (UTR)
Every NEFT and RTGS transaction generates a UTR number (e.g., AXISN12345...) for tracking. Both sender and beneficiary can use UTR to investigate transaction status.

IMPS — IMMEDIATE PAYMENT SERVICE

What it is: NPCI's 24×7 real-time inter-bank fund transfer service launched in 2010. Operates on a different rail from NEFT/RTGS.

Settlement model: Real-time, similar to RTGS but with significantly lower transaction limit and serving retail.

Availability: 24×7×365 since launch.

Transaction limit:
- Per transaction: ₹5 lakh (NPCI ceiling, raised from ₹2 lakh in 2021)
- Per day: ₹5 lakh (varies by bank — many cap at ₹2 lakh or ₹3 lakh per day for retail)

Charges:
- Online via mobile/net banking: ₹2.50-₹15 per transaction (banks vary)
- Slab-based:
  - Up to ₹1,000: ₹2.50
  - ₹1,001-₹10,000: ₹3-₹5
  - ₹10,001-₹1 lakh: ₹5-₹15
  - ₹1 lakh-₹5 lakh: ₹15-₹25
- Plus GST 18%

Processing time:
- Instant — credit to beneficiary within seconds
- Confirmation SMS to both parties

Information needed:
- IMPS via Account number + IFSC (P2A — Person to Account)
- IMPS via Mobile + MMID (Mobile Money Identifier) — older method, 7-digit MMID
- Beneficiary registration cooling period applies similar to NEFT

Use cases:
- Mid-value retail transfers (₹10,000-₹5 lakh)
- Urgent transfers outside UPI limits
- Salary payments by small businesses where UPI not used
- Travel agents, real estate brokers receiving payments

COMPARISON TABLE — NEFT vs. RTGS vs. IMPS vs. UPI

| Parameter | NEFT | RTGS | IMPS | UPI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Operator | RBI | RBI | NPCI | NPCI |
| Speed | 30 min-2 hr batches | Real-time | Real-time | Instant |
| 24×7 | Yes (since Dec 2019) | Yes (since Dec 2020) | Yes | Yes |
| Min amount | No min | ₹2,00,000 | No min | No min |
| Max amount | No max (RBI) | No max | ₹5 lakh | ₹1 lakh (₹2-5 lakh special) |

WHEN TO USE WHAT

Use UPI when:
- Transferring up to ₹1 lakh (₹2-5 lakh for special categories)
- Paying merchants via QR
- Daily, frequent small transfers
- Convenience is paramount

Use IMPS when:
- ₹1 lakh-₹5 lakh urgent transfer
- Need real-time confirmation
- UPI limit insufficient

Use NEFT when:
- Routine transfers, no rush
- Want zero charges and beneficiary account+IFSC method
- Salary disbursement, vendor payments by small businesses

Use RTGS when:
- ₹2 lakh+ transfer needed quickly
- High-value: property, large investments
- Want gross real-time settlement

NEFT BATCHES TIMING (until 2019)
NEFT used to operate in hourly batches during banking hours only. Post Dec 2019, half-hourly batches 24×7. Settlement times:
- 00:30 hours, 01:00 hours, 01:30 hours, ... every 30 minutes round the clock
- Last batch at 24:00 hours

REJECTION OR FAILED TRANSACTION
Common reasons:
- Incorrect beneficiary account number
- Wrong IFSC code
- Beneficiary account frozen/closed
- Bank server issue at beneficiary side
- Account number format mismatch

In such cases:
- Amount auto-credited back to remitter's account within 2 business hours (RTGS) or 4 hours (NEFT)
- For IMPS: instant auto-reversal
- If not reversed within 24 hours, raise complaint with home branch
- RBI mandates resolution within reasonable time; otherwise penal interest applies

TRACKING A TRANSFER
- Note the UTR / RRN / Reference number at time of transaction
- Net banking → Transaction inquiry / Status
- Customer care: Provide UTR and date
- For ambiguous status >24 hours, raise dispute formally

GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
- Approach branch / customer care first
- Escalate to Nodal Officer
- RBI Banking Ombudsman via cms.rbi.org.in if unresolved in 30 days
- Mandatory turn-around-time and penal interest if bank delays

CHEQUE-BASED NEFT / RTGS AT BRANCH
For non-account holders or cash-based:
- Deposit cash and instruction at any bank branch (some banks)
- Up to ₹50,000 for NEFT
- Pay branch charges + GST
- Slower process: branch issues transaction by next batch

BULK TRANSFERS (FOR BUSINESSES)
Corporate net banking supports bulk file upload:
- CSV/Excel file with beneficiary details and amounts
- Approval workflow (maker-checker)
- Used for salary, vendor batch payments
- Bulk NEFT or RTGS depending on amounts and urgency

INTERNATIONAL EQUIVALENTS
- SWIFT for international wire transfers (slower, costs higher fee)
- Cross-border UPI in select countries
- RemitNow / outward remittance via banks for foreign transfers under LRS
- Wire transfer for inward foreign remittance

CHARGES SUMMARY (FY 2024-25 INDICATIVE)
| Channel | NEFT | RTGS | IMPS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Net banking / mobile | FREE | FREE | ₹2.50-₹25 |
| Branch (with account) | ₹2.50-₹25 | ₹25-₹50 | NA mostly |
| Branch (walk-in/cash) | ₹25-₹50 | NA | NA |

Plus GST 18% on charges.

KEY RBI INITIATIVES TO MAKE TRANSFERS FREE
- 2020: NEFT and RTGS online charges waived
- 2019-2020: NEFT/RTGS 24×7 enabled
- 2021: IMPS limit raised to ₹5 lakh
- 2023-24: UPI limit raised for specific use cases

USE-CASE SCENARIOS

Scenario 1: Pay ₹50,000 rent to landlord — Use UPI (free, instant)

Scenario 2: Send ₹3 lakh to friend for emergency — Use IMPS (instant) or NEFT (free)

Scenario 3: Pay ₹50 lakh property down payment — Use RTGS (real-time, no charge online)

Scenario 4: Pay ₹15,000 vendor invoice for business — NEFT (free)

Scenario 5: Salary disbursement for 100 employees — Bulk NEFT via corporate net banking

Rates and limits may change, verify with bank — RBI / NPCI revise these periodically. Always check current schedule of charges on your bank's website.
