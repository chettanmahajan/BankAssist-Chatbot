UPI (UNIFIED PAYMENTS INTERFACE) — COMPLETE GUIDE

OVERVIEW
The Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is India's revolutionary real-time inter-bank payment system, developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) and launched in April 2016. UPI allows instant money transfer between bank accounts using a smartphone, 24×7, including holidays, with no transfer fee for personal transactions. UPI has transformed payments in India and is now the world's largest real-time payment system by transaction volume. Major UPI apps include PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, BHIM, Amazon Pay, WhatsApp Pay, and bank-specific apps from SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, and others. Rates and limits are indicative; verify with bank/NPCI for latest.

HOW UPI WORKS
UPI uses a unique identifier called Virtual Payment Address (VPA) or UPI ID — looks like name@bank or name@upi (e.g., chetan@okhdfcbank, 9876543210@paytm). To send money:
1. Open UPI app, link your bank account (one-time setup with debit card details to set UPI PIN)
2. Enter recipient's UPI ID, bank account + IFSC, mobile number, or scan QR code
3. Enter amount and note (optional)
4. Authorise with your 4-6 digit UPI PIN
5. Money debited from your bank account and credited to recipient's bank account instantly
6. Both sender and recipient receive SMS confirmation

UPI works on the IMPS (Immediate Payment Service) rails managed by NPCI for instant settlement. Unlike NEFT/RTGS, UPI does not require entering long account numbers and IFSC codes — VPA / mobile / QR is enough.

UPI LIMITS (INDICATIVE)
NPCI sets transaction limits and banks may apply lower internal limits.

Per Transaction Limit:
- Standard: ₹1,00,000 per transaction
- Capital markets (mutual funds, brokers, insurance, credit card payment to own card): ₹2,00,000
- IPO subscriptions, retail direct G-sec: ₹5,00,000
- Education and healthcare payments to verified merchants: ₹5,00,000 (raised in late 2023)
- Tax payments (income tax, GST): ₹5,00,000 per transaction

Daily Limit:
- Standard: ₹1,00,000 cumulative (across all UPI transactions in a day)
- Banks may have lower limits for new customers / new VPAs (₹5,000-₹25,000 first 24 hours)
- Some banks allow ₹2,00,000 daily for verified customers

UPI 123Pay (for feature phones):
- ₹10,000 per transaction
- Uses IVR (calling a specific number) instead of app — for non-smartphone users

UPI LITE (small-value, low-friction):
- Wallet within UPI, pre-loaded up to ₹2,000
- Per transaction ₹500 (raised to ₹1,000 in some banks)
- Daily debit ₹4,000 from UPI Lite wallet
- No UPI PIN required for these small transactions (already authorised at wallet load stage)
- Reduces server load and fits offline scenarios

NUMBER OF TRANSACTIONS:
No NPCI limit on count; banks may apply 10-20 transactions per day per VPA for fraud control.

P2P (PEER-TO-PEER) PAYMENTS
- Free for sender and recipient (RBI/NPCI mandate — no merchant discount rate on P2P)
- Bank-to-bank settlement instant
- Cannot be reversed once successful — verify recipient before authorising

P2M (PEER-TO-MERCHANT) PAYMENTS
- Free for users on UPI (no MDR on UPI payments to merchants as per government waiver effective 2020)
- Merchants accept via QR code (BharatQR / static / dynamic UPI QR)
- Merchant fees: Earlier 0%, government subsidises through PIDF / DSF
- Merchants get instant credit in their bank account

UPI AUTOPAY
A recurring mandate facility introduced in 2020. Allows users to authorise periodic auto-payments:
- Subscription services: Netflix, Amazon Prime, Spotify, Hotstar
- Bills: Electricity, gas, water, mobile, broadband
- Insurance premiums
- Mutual fund SIPs
- Loan EMIs (some banks)
- OTT and digital subscriptions

How it works:
1. Merchant requests AutoPay mandate during checkout
2. User authorises one-time with UPI PIN
3. Mandate created — specifies max amount, frequency (daily/weekly/monthly), validity
4. On each due date, debit happens automatically, with 24-hour pre-debit notification to user
5. User can cancel mandate anytime from UPI app
- Max amount: ₹15,000 per AutoPay without additional authentication (raised from ₹5,000)
- Above ₹15,000: Two-factor authentication for each debit

UPI ON CREDIT CARDS (UPI CC)
Launched in 2022, allowing credit cards to be linked to UPI apps. Currently supports RuPay credit cards from select banks (HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, Yes, IDFC, Canara, etc.). Features:
- Pay merchants via UPI but charged to credit card (not bank account)
- Earn credit card reward points (some cards exclude UPI-CC from rewards)
- 30-50 day interest-free credit
- MDR applies on merchant side for amounts above ₹2,000 (small merchants exempt); 1.1% capped
- Useful for users wanting credit card convenience with UPI's wide acceptance
- Daily limit similar to UPI standard

UPI INTERNATIONAL
UPI has been rolled out internationally for:
- Inbound foreign tourists to India (Singapore, UAE, France, Mauritius, Bhutan, Nepal, Sri Lanka linkages)
- Indian travelers can pay abroad using UPI in select countries (Singapore PayNow integration, France Eiffel Tower pilot, etc.)
- NRI accounts: NRE accounts can use UPI for inward/outward transfers (subject to bank enablement)

KEY UPI APPS
1. PhonePe: Largest market share; strong merchant network; investment products
2. Google Pay: Second largest; strong UX; rewards via scratch cards
3. Paytm: Wallet + UPI + payments bank; merchant focus
4. BHIM: NPCI's official app; basic but reliable; supported by government
5. Amazon Pay: Bundled with Amazon shopping; cashback offers
6. WhatsApp Pay: Within WhatsApp; convenient but slower adoption
7. Bank apps: SBI YONO, HDFC PayZapp, ICICI iMobile, Axis Mobile, Kotak Mobile, etc.

SECURITY FEATURES
- UPI PIN: 4-6 digit secret entered at app, never typed at merchant
- Device binding: UPI registered to specific mobile number + device; new device requires re-registration
- SMS to registered number for UPI ID setup and PIN change
- Transaction notifications via app, SMS, email
- Fraud detection: NPCI's central monitoring flags suspicious patterns
- Cooling period for new VPA / device: lower limits for 24-72 hours

UPI PIN
- Set at registration using debit card last 6 digits + expiry + OTP, OR Aadhaar-based OTP
- Used to authorise every UPI debit (except UPI Lite small amounts)
- Can be changed anytime via app
- Forgot PIN: Reset via app → bank verifies via debit card or Aadhaar

QR CODES
- Static QR: Permanent printed code at merchant for accepting payments — buyer scans, enters amount, pays
- Dynamic QR: Generated for specific transaction amount — buyer just scans and authorises
- BharatQR: Interoperable QR standard supporting UPI, RuPay, Visa, Mastercard, Amex
- UPI QR: NPCI-specific; UPI-only

UPI vs. IMPS vs. NEFT vs. RTGS — QUICK COMPARISON
| Feature | UPI | IMPS | NEFT | RTGS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Speed | Instant | Instant | 30 min-batch | Instant (real-time) |
| Availability | 24×7 | 24×7 | 24×7 (since Dec 2019) | 24×7 (since Dec 2020) |
| Limit per txn | ₹1 lakh-₹5 lakh | ₹5 lakh | No limit (₹50,000 for first txn to new beneficiary at branch) | ₹2 lakh minimum, no upper |
| Charges | Free | ₹2-₹15 | Free (online) | Free (online) |
| Identifier | VPA/QR/mobile | Mobile + MMID, or Acc + IFSC | Acc + IFSC | Acc + IFSC |
| Use case | Daily payments, retail | Mid-value, urgent | Routine retail | Large-value (₹2 lakh+) |

UPI FOR MERCHANTS
- Free acceptance for transactions up to ₹2,000 (MDR fully borne by government / waived)
- Above ₹2,000: 0% on RuPay/UPI P2M (government waived MDR for UPI)
- Instant settlement to merchant bank account
- No POS hardware needed (QR code suffices)
- SoundBox/SmartSpeaker devices announce payment received (Paytm SoundBox, PhonePe Speaker)
- Reconciliation tools via app or merchant portal
- Settlement reports for accounting

UPI FAILED / PENDING TRANSACTIONS
If amount is debited but not credited to recipient:
- Most transactions auto-reverse within minutes
- For pending status (over 24 hours): Raise dispute in UPI app; bank investigates
- NPCI Online Dispute Resolution (UDIR) framework ensures complaints are addressed within T+3 days
- Mandatory chargeback timeline: T+5 days for refund

UPI CHARGES (CURRENT STATUS FY 2024-25)
- P2P (personal): FREE
- P2M up to ₹2,000 via PPI (Prepaid Payment Instrument like Paytm wallet) loaded on UPI: 1.1% interchange (paid by merchant, not user)
- Standard P2M (bank account → merchant UPI ID): FREE (no MDR)
- UPI on credit cards (UPI-CC): MDR applies on merchant for transactions above ₹2,000 (small merchant exemption)

CHALLENGES AND COMMON ISSUES
- Bank server down: Transaction fails or pending; retry or switch bank app
- VPA blocked: Contact bank, may be due to suspicious activity flag
- PIN forgotten: Reset via debit card details
- Wrong UPI ID typed: Money may be credited to a different person; raise dispute, but reversal depends on recipient
- App registration not working: Mobile number may not be registered with bank; visit branch to update
- Transaction limit hit: Wait for next day or use NEFT/RTGS

GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES PROMOTING UPI
- Zero MDR on UPI: Effective Jan 2020
- BHIM Aadhaar Pay: Cash withdrawal at merchant via Aadhaar fingerprint
- DigiDhan Mission: Promoting digital payments
- ONDC integration with UPI for e-commerce
- Cross-border UPI rollout
- UPI 2.0, UPI Lite, UPI 123Pay expansions

PRECAUTIONS / SAFETY TIPS
1. Never share UPI PIN with anyone — banks/UPI apps NEVER ask
2. Verify recipient before sending — name displayed should match expectation
3. Don't enter UPI PIN to "receive" money — UPI PIN is only for sending, never receiving
4. Beware of fake QR codes pasted over genuine ones
5. Don't scan QR codes from unknown sources
6. Don't click links in suspicious SMS/WhatsApp claiming UPI rewards or refunds
7. Enable transaction alerts (SMS + email)
8. Set UPI app lock with biometric / PIN within the app
9. Don't store screenshots of UPI PIN or VPA setup
10. Report fraud immediately to bank and cybercrime.gov.in / 1930

UPI is free, instant, secure, and ubiquitous — but only as safe as your own vigilance. Rates and limits may change, verify with bank/NPCI for current details.
