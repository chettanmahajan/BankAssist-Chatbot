ATM AND DEBIT CARD — LIMITS, CHARGES, SECURITY

OVERVIEW
An ATM (Automated Teller Machine) is an electronic banking outlet that allows customers to perform basic transactions — cash withdrawal, balance inquiry, mini-statement, fund transfer, PIN change, cardless cash withdrawal — without visiting a teller. The Debit Card is the primary instrument for ATM access and is also used for point-of-sale (POS) purchases, online transactions, and contactless tap-and-pay. RBI regulates ATM operations and debit card issuance through dedicated guidelines including Master Direction on Issuance and Conduct of Debit and Credit Cards, 2022. Rates and figures are indicative; rates may change, verify with bank.

DEBIT CARD VARIANTS
Indian banks issue debit cards under three primary card networks: Visa, Mastercard, and RuPay (NPCI's domestic network).

Standard variants by tier:
1. Classic / Silver: Entry-level — ATM withdrawal ₹25,000-₹40,000/day, POS ₹1 lakh/day
2. Gold / Titanium: Mid-tier — ATM ₹50,000/day, POS ₹1-2 lakh/day, basic insurance, online accident cover
3. Platinum: Premium — ATM ₹50,000-₹1 lakh/day, POS ₹2-5 lakh/day, lounge access, concierge
4. Signature / World / Business: Super-premium — ATM ₹1-2 lakh/day, POS ₹5-10 lakh/day, lounge access, golf

Special variants:
- Women's debit card (Pearl, Nari, Mahila, etc.)
- Senior citizen debit card
- Business debit card (for current account)
- BSBDA RuPay (free debit card for Jan Dhan account)
- Co-branded debit cards (e.g., Air India SBI Debit Card, Tata Croma debit cards)
- Virtual debit card (digital-only)

RUPAY — DOMESTIC NETWORK
- NPCI's payment network, launched 2012
- Mandatory for Jan Dhan and various government schemes
- Lower transaction fees → preferred by banks
- Pioneer in domestic UPI on credit cards
- International acceptance via tie-ups (UnionPay, Discover, Diners)
- Often comes with bundled accident insurance: ₹1-2 lakh (basic), up to ₹10 lakh (premium variants)

ATM WITHDRAWAL LIMITS (TYPICAL)
- Per transaction: ₹10,000-₹20,000 (some ATMs up to ₹40,000)
- Per day: Per debit card variant
  - Classic: ₹25,000-₹40,000
  - Gold: ₹50,000
  - Platinum: ₹50,000-₹1,00,000
  - Premium: ₹1,00,000-₹2,00,000

You can configure daily ATM limit (within bank-set max) via net banking or app.

ATM WITHDRAWAL CHARGES — RBI RULES (Updated 2022)

Free transactions per month per RBI:
- At own bank's ATM (any city): 5 free per month (financial + non-financial)
- At other bank's ATM:
  - Metro (Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Hyderabad): 3 free per month
  - Non-metro: 5 free per month

After free limit, banks charge customer:
- Up to June 2024: ₹21 per financial transaction + ₹10 per non-financial (balance inquiry, mini-statement, PIN change)
- From May 1, 2025 (revised by RBI): ₹23 per financial transaction (raised from ₹21)
- Plus GST 18%

Non-financial transactions at ATM:
- Balance inquiry: ₹6-₹10 after free limit
- Mini-statement: ₹6-₹10
- PIN change: Usually free at own bank; ₹6-₹10 at other bank

Note: BSBDA / Jan Dhan accounts have separate (often slightly different) ATM charge structure.

POS / ONLINE TRANSACTION LIMITS
- Daily POS purchase: ₹1-₹6 lakh based on card variant
- Daily online transaction: Same range or set separately
- International usage: By default disabled (RBI mandate); enable via app/net banking with custom limit
- Contactless tap-to-pay limit without PIN: ₹5,000 per transaction (RBI raised from ₹2,000 in March 2020); above ₹5,000 requires PIN

DEBIT CARD CHARGES — ANNUAL AND ISSUANCE

Issuance fee:
- Classic: ₹100-₹200 (often free for new accounts)
- Gold/Platinum: ₹300-₹500
- Signature/Business: ₹500-₹1,500

Annual maintenance fee:
- Classic: ₹100-₹250
- Gold: ₹250-₹500
- Platinum: ₹500-₹1,000
- Premium: ₹750-₹1,500

Replacement card: ₹100-₹500 (free in case of damage on first request, sometimes)
Duplicate PIN: ₹50-₹100 via branch; usually free via app/net banking OTP-based generation
GST 18% on all card-related fees.

Note: BSBDA / Jan Dhan RuPay debit card is free for life.

ATM USAGE — STEP BY STEP

Cash withdrawal:
1. Insert debit card (chip side up, name side first in modern ATMs)
2. Select language
3. Enter 4-digit PIN (cover the keypad)
4. Choose "Cash Withdrawal" → Account type (Savings/Current)
5. Enter amount (multiples of ₹100 typically)
6. Collect cash, card, and receipt
7. Press cancel/end to clear the session

If cash not dispensed but account debited:
- Usually auto-reversed within 5 working days (RBI norm)
- If not, contact bank with ATM ID (printed on receipt), date, time, amount
- Bank investigates with the ATM operator (e.g., NCR, FSS, Hitachi)
- Compensation of ₹100 per day after 5 working days if delay continues (RBI mandate)

Balance inquiry:
- Insert card → PIN → Balance Inquiry → Choose account → View on-screen / print mini-statement
- Counts as non-financial transaction

PIN change:
- Insert card → PIN → PIN Change → Enter new PIN twice → Confirm
- Free at own bank ATM; small charge at other bank

Cash deposit at CDM (Cash Deposit Machine):
- Insert card → PIN → Cash Deposit → Account type → Insert notes
- Machine counts and credits to account instantly
- Limit: ₹49,900 per transaction (PAN required for ₹50,000+); ₹2 lakh per day
- No charge usually

Cardless cash withdrawal:
- Generate OTP/QR via bank's mobile app
- At enabled ATM: Select Cardless option → Enter mobile + OTP → Withdraw
- Useful when card is lost/forgotten

UPI ATM (NEW FEATURE — 2023)
NPCI's interoperable cardless cash withdrawal via UPI:
- At participating ATM, select UPI ATM → Scan QR with UPI app
- Authorise with UPI PIN → Cash dispensed
- Cumulative daily limit: Same as UPI daily cap
- No card needed; works with any UPI app

CARD SECURITY FEATURES

1. EMV Chip: Encrypted chip generates unique cryptogram per transaction; cloning very difficult
2. PIN: 4-digit secret; entered at ATM, POS, sensitive online transactions
3. CVV / CVV2: 3-digit code on back of card for online transactions
4. Holographic stripe: Anti-counterfeiting feature
5. Tokenisation: Card details replaced with merchant-specific token (RBI mandate effective Oct 2022)
6. Contactless: Cryptographic NFC chip; ₹5,000 limit without PIN
7. International usage: Disabled by default; enable selectively

DEBIT CARD CONTROLS (VIA APP)
Real-time card management:
- Block / unblock card
- Generate / regenerate PIN
- Enable/disable: ATM, POS, online, international, contactless
- Set transaction limits per channel
- Set daily and monthly cap
- View pending and posted transactions
- Lost card report and replacement request
- Dispute transactions directly

LOST CARD — IMMEDIATE STEPS
1. Block the card via mobile app / 24×7 helpline / SMS short code / net banking
2. Get confirmation reference number
3. File FIR if physically stolen
4. Request replacement (delivery in 7-10 days; instant virtual card via app if supported)
5. Monitor account for any unauthorised transactions
6. Report any unauthorised transactions within 3 working days for zero liability

FRAUD AND ZERO-LIABILITY (RBI CIRCULAR ON LIMITED LIABILITY OF CUSTOMERS)
Same framework as credit card (see credit_card_security.txt):
- Bank fault: Zero liability irrespective of timing
- Third-party fraud, no fault of customer/bank: 0 liability if reported within 3 working days; ₹10,000-₹25,000 if 4-7 days; bank's discretion thereafter
- Customer's negligence (shared PIN): Customer liable until reported; zero after reporting

DAY-TO-DAY ATM SECURITY TIPS
1. Cover the keypad when entering PIN
2. Use familiar ATMs (bank lobby is safer than standalone)
3. Inspect the card slot before inserting (skimmer detection)
4. Be alert for "shoulder surfers"
5. Never accept "help" from strangers at the ATM
6. Don't write PIN on the card or store with the card
7. If card is captured by ATM, call the bank's helpline immediately
8. Check SMS alerts for every transaction
9. Reconcile bank statement monthly for any anomalies
10. Avoid using ATM in poorly lit or isolated locations late at night

ATM TYPES
1. Bank ATM: Operated by the bank itself (most common)
2. White Label ATM (WLA): Operated by non-bank entities like Tata Indicash, BTI Payments — accept all bank cards
3. Brown Label ATM: Hardware owned by bank, operations outsourced
4. CDM: Cash Deposit Machine (deposit cash)
5. ATM cum Cash Recycler: Both deposit and withdrawal
6. Smart ATM: Additional services — passbook update, cheque deposit, NEFT, FD opening

NON-FINANCIAL TRANSACTIONS AT ATM
- Balance inquiry
- Mini-statement (last 10 transactions)
- PIN change
- Cheque book request
- Statement request
- Standing instruction setup (in advanced ATMs)
- Mobile recharge (in advanced ATMs)
- Beneficiary transfer (some ATMs)
- Aadhaar linking confirmation

DEBIT CARD BENEFITS (BUNDLED INSURANCE)
Many debit cards bundle complimentary insurance:
- Personal accident cover: ₹1 lakh-₹10 lakh
- Air accident cover: ₹2 lakh-₹50 lakh (on Premium variants)
- Purchase protection / lost card liability cover: ₹50,000-₹3 lakh
- Hospital cash, ER coverage in some premium variants
RuPay debit cards under PMJDY: ₹2 lakh accident insurance

REWARDS AND CASHBACK ON DEBIT CARDS
Some debit cards offer rewards on transactions:
- 1%-5% cashback on select categories (online, dining, fuel)
- Reward points redeemable for vouchers / products
- Domestic lounge access on Premium variants (1-4 visits per quarter)
- Some cards offer milestone-based bonus rewards
Examples: HDFC Millennia debit card, Axis Atlas debit card, ICICI Coral debit card

VIRTUAL DEBIT CARD
Some banks now offer instant virtual debit cards:
- Generated in app within seconds
- Used for online transactions
- Can be cancelled and regenerated
- Higher security as separate number from physical card
- Useful for one-time online use

INTERNATIONAL USAGE
- Default: Disabled
- Enable via app: Set per-day usage cap, validity period
- Forex markup: Typically 3.5% on debit card international transactions (higher than credit card)
- Some banks: 1.5%-2% forex markup on premium variants
- Use Forex Card (loaded with foreign currency) for cheaper international spending
- Niyo Global debit card: 0% forex markup with locked-in rate

WITHDRAWAL LIMITS — RBI MAXIMUM
- RBI permits up to ₹2 lakh per day for ATM withdrawal per card (banks set lower limits)
- Per transaction: Bank ATM hardware limit, typically ₹10,000-₹40,000

KYC AND CARD ISSUANCE
- Card linked to KYC-compliant account
- New card issuance: Within 7 days of account opening
- Re-issuance on expiry: Auto-renewed 30 days before expiry
- Lost / damage replacement: 7-10 days; instant via app if virtual supported

DORMANT CARD
If debit card not used for 12-18 months:
- Some banks deactivate; reactivation needs branch visit or app
- Some banks reissue at expiry only if usage detected

DEBIT CARD VS. CREDIT CARD — QUICK COMPARISON
| Feature | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Funds | Own | Bank's credit |
| Interest | None | 36%-48% if unpaid post due |
| Rewards | Limited | Higher |
| Building Credit Score | No | Yes |
| Bills | None (immediate debit) | Monthly statement |
| Online security | OTP + PIN | OTP + 3D Secure |
| Forex markup | 3.5%+ typical | 1.5%-3.5% |

Rates may change, verify with bank — debit card features, charges, and limits are reviewed periodically.
