NRI REMITTANCE AND FOREIGN EXCHANGE — INWARD AND OUTWARD GUIDE

OVERVIEW
Remittance is the transfer of money across international borders. India is the world's largest recipient of inward remittance, receiving over USD 125 billion in FY 2023-24. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI), under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999, regulates all cross-border money flows. Banks are the primary channel for inward and outward remittance through authorised dealer (AD) licences. All figures are indicative; rates may change, verify with bank / RBI.

INWARD REMITTANCE (FOREIGN TO INDIA)

Common Reasons for Inward Remittance:
- Family maintenance / financial support to parents and dependents
- Funding NRE / FCNR / NRO accounts
- Investment in India (real estate, MF, equity)
- EMI repayment for Indian loans
- Education or medical expenses for self/family in India
- Personal expense reimbursement
- Charitable donations to Indian NGOs

Methods of Inward Remittance:

1. Wire Transfer (SWIFT)
Most common for large amounts:
- Sender initiates transfer from foreign bank to Indian bank using SWIFT network
- Foreign bank's fee: USD 20-50 (some premium banks waive)
- Intermediary bank fee: USD 10-30 (correspondent banks)
- Indian bank's fee: ₹250-₹500 + GST (some banks free for NRE deposits)
- Time: 1-3 working days
- Exchange rate: Bank's prevailing rate plus 1%-2.5% markup over interbank rate

Required:
- IFSC code of beneficiary bank in India
- Account number
- Beneficiary name
- Beneficiary bank's SWIFT code (e.g., HDFCINBB, SBININBB)
- Beneficiary's full address

2. Online Money Transfer Services
Cheaper and faster than wire transfer for amounts up to USD 50,000:
- Wise (formerly TransferWise): Mid-market rate + transparent low fee (often 0.5%-1% all-in cost)
- Remitly: Strong India corridor; multiple speed tiers
- Western Union: Vast cash pickup network
- MoneyGram: Similar to Western Union
- Xoom (PayPal subsidiary): Online + agent pickup
- WorldRemit: Competitive rates

Time: Minutes to 24 hours
Fee: Often free or USD 0-5 for digital-to-digital
Markup: 0.5%-1.5%

3. Bank-Specific Remittance Platforms
Major Indian banks operate dedicated remittance platforms with foreign banking partners:
- SBI ExpressRemit
- HDFC Bank RemitNow
- ICICI Bank Money2India
- Axis Bank RemitMoney
- Kotak Mahindra Click2Remit
- Yes Bank Remit
- IDFC FIRST Bank Remittance

Features:
- Online tracking
- Better rates than third-party for the bank's home corridor
- Direct deposit to NRE/NRO/Resident accounts
- Some are free; others have nominal fee

4. UPI International (NEW)
Limited but expanding: Cross-border UPI for inbound payments in some countries (Singapore PayNow, UAE, France, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Mauritius integration). Indian merchants can receive payment from foreign tourists via UPI. Currently for retail transactions, not large-value remittance.

5. Foreign Currency Demand Draft (FC DD)
Largely obsolete due to costs and time; some still use:
- Issued by foreign bank to Indian bank
- Mailed physically
- Encashed at Indian bank's foreign exchange department
- Time: 7-21 days

6. Cash Pickup (Western Union / MoneyGram)
Useful when receiver doesn't have bank account or needs quick cash:
- Sender pays in their country with cash or card
- Receiver picks up cash at agent location in India with PIN
- Limited to USD 2,500 per transaction (or equivalent) per RBI

CHARGES FOR INWARD REMITTANCE (RECEIVING SIDE)
- To NRE / NRO / Resident account: ₹0-₹500 + GST (depends on bank)
- Premium NRI accounts: Often free
- FIRA (Foreign Inward Remittance Advice / Certificate): Free or ₹100-₹500 per request — required for some legal/tax purposes

EXCHANGE RATE
When foreign currency is credited to INR account:
- Bank converts at its "TT Buying Rate" (Telegraphic Transfer Buying Rate) for inward remittance
- TT Buying < Spot Rate < TT Selling (typically 1%-2% spread)
- The spread is the bank's profit margin
- Online comparison tools / Wise show mid-market rate for reference

For example, if interbank USD/INR = ₹83.50:
- TT Buying (inward) ≈ ₹83.00-₹83.30 (1-2% below mid)
- TT Selling (outward) ≈ ₹83.70-₹84.00 (1-2% above mid)
- Conversion charge / forex markup may add another 0.5%-1.5%

REPATRIATION (OUTWARD FROM INDIA TO ABROAD)

From NRE / FCNR accounts:
- FREELY REPATRIABLE
- No upper limit
- Principal and interest both
- No tax (since interest is already tax-free)

From NRO account:
- Limited to USD 1,000,000 (one million) per financial year (April-March)
- Combined of current income (interest, rent, dividends) and capital
- After Indian tax compliance:
  - Form 15CA (self-declaration by remitter regarding tax compliance)
  - Form 15CB (CA-certified certificate confirming tax compliance) — for amounts above ₹5 lakh / specific categories
  - Required for all remittances except some exempt categories
- TDS withheld at applicable rates on relevant payments

From Resident Indian accounts (under LRS):
- See Liberalised Remittance Scheme below

LIBERALISED REMITTANCE SCHEME (LRS)
RBI's scheme allowing Resident Indians (NOT NRIs — they use NRO/NRE) to remit up to USD 2,50,000 per financial year for permitted purposes:

Permitted Uses:
- Travel abroad (private + business)
- Studies abroad (tuition + living)
- Medical treatment
- Maintenance of close relatives abroad
- Gifts and donations
- Investment in foreign equity, bonds, mutual funds
- Acquisition of foreign property
- Foreign currency loans to NRIs
- Forex card top-up

NOT Permitted:
- Margin trading / leveraged trades
- Crypto / virtual digital asset purchase (RBI/IT clarification varies; check current rules)
- Lottery, bookmaking, prohibited activities
- Foreign exchange trading outside India
- Sweepstakes / casino gambling
- Purchases from FATF non-compliant countries

Documentation:
- PAN mandatory
- Form A2 (foreign exchange declaration)
- Supporting documents (visa, course offer, treatment estimate, etc.) based on purpose
- TCS (Tax Collected at Source) at 20% on remittance above ₹7 lakh in a year for certain purposes (raised from 5%) effective 1 October 2023; refundable / creditable against income tax liability
- Specific TCS rates:
  - Education (with loan from financial institution): 0.5%
  - Education (own funds) and medical: 5% above ₹7 lakh
  - Tour packages: 5% up to ₹7 lakh, 20% above
  - Other: 20% on entire amount above ₹7 lakh
  - Investment in foreign assets: 20% above ₹7 lakh

LRS Aggregation:
- Across all banks (RBI consolidates via FETERS)
- Per individual (not family)
- HUF: USD 250,000 per family member

FOREX CARDS
A pre-paid card loaded with foreign currency for international travel — more convenient and cheaper than using debit/credit card abroad.

Types:
- Single Currency Card: Loaded with one currency (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.)
- Multi-Currency Card: Holds multiple currencies in separate wallets (USD + EUR + GBP + JPY + SGD etc.)

Features:
- Locked exchange rate at time of loading
- No surprise forex markup at usage
- Replacement card if lost (online emergency funds)
- ATM withdrawal abroad (USD 2.5-5 per transaction fee)
- POS purchases free (no markup)

Issuance:
- Banks: HDFC Multicurrency Forex Plus, Axis Multi-Currency, ICICI Travel Card, SBI Vyaapaar (business)
- Fintech: Niyo Global Card (excellent rates), BookMyForex
- Limit: USD 2,50,000 LRS cap

Charges:
- Issuance fee: ₹150-₹500
- Reload fee: ₹50-₹150 per reload
- Conversion fee: 0%-1% on currency conversion within card
- ATM withdrawal fee abroad: USD 2.5-5 per transaction
- Annual maintenance: Often free

For frequent international travel, Forex card is highly recommended over credit/debit card.

GIFT TO RESIDENT FROM NRI
- Cash gift up to USD 250,000 per year (LRS for NRI giving) — limit applies on giver's side
- No tax to recipient if gift from close relative; otherwise taxable as "Income from Other Sources" if > ₹50,000 in a year (Indian gift tax rule)
- Documents: Gift deed (recommended for large gifts)

FORM 15CA / 15CB DEEP-DIVE

Form 15CA: Self-declaration by remitter regarding nature of payment and tax compliance. Filed online at Income Tax e-filing portal.

Parts of 15CA:
- Part A: Remittances ≤ ₹5 lakh, no Form 15CB needed
- Part B: Remittances above ₹5 lakh that don't qualify under Rule 37BB (taxable), Form 15CB needed
- Part C: Remittances above ₹5 lakh where 15CB is mandatory
- Part D: Remittances of specified nature exempt from CA certification (e.g., certain LRS heads)

Form 15CB: CA's certificate confirming:
- Nature and purpose of remittance
- Applicability of TDS
- DTAA benefits
- Tax compliance
- Forwarded to bank along with Form 15CA acknowledgment

Cost: CA fee ₹2,000-₹10,000+ depending on complexity.

TCS — TAX COLLECTED AT SOURCE ON LRS
Effective 1 October 2023:
- Threshold: ₹7 lakh per FY (cumulative across banks)
- Rate: 20% on amount above ₹7 lakh (for most purposes)
- Exceptions:
  - Education with loan: 0.5%
  - Education / medical (own funds): 5% above ₹7 lakh
  - Tour packages: 5% up to ₹7 lakh, 20% above
- TCS deducted by remitting bank
- Reflected in Form 26AS / AIS
- Refundable / adjustable against income tax liability at year-end

COMMON DOCUMENTATION FOR INWARD/OUTWARD

Inward (no purpose-specific documents usually needed, only KYC):
- Beneficiary bank details
- Sender's source-of-funds declaration for large amounts (anti-money-laundering)

Outward (purpose-based):
- Travel: Visa, ticket
- Education: Admission letter, fee structure
- Medical: Doctor's prescription / hospital estimate
- Investment: Brokerage / fund details
- Property: Purchase agreement, property title docs
- LRS Form A2 always required

FATCA / CRS COMPLIANCE
Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (USA) and Common Reporting Standard (OECD):
- Indian banks must report accounts of US persons (FATCA) and tax residents of other CRS jurisdictions
- NRIs must declare tax residency in account opening forms
- Cross-border reporting between tax authorities reduces tax evasion

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS BY COUNTRY

USA:
- FATCA applicable
- DTAA available
- TRC + Form 10F required for DTAA benefit
- Many Indian mutual funds restrict US-based NRIs due to FATCA reporting burden

UAE:
- Large NRI population (Indian diaspora)
- DTAA available
- No income tax in UAE; income from UAE not double-taxed
- Common remittance corridor with strong banking partnerships

UK / EU:
- DTAA with most countries
- Brexit-related compliance for UK

Gulf Countries (Saudi, Kuwait, Qatar):
- Large remittance corridor
- Tax-free salary in source country
- DTAA available

MISTAKES TO AVOID
1. Mixing source of funds in NRE/NRO (one is for foreign, other for Indian income)
2. Not informing bank on residency change
3. Forgetting to claim DTAA benefits
4. Ignoring TCS on LRS that could be saved with proper structuring
5. Not retaining FIRA / SWIFT receipt for tax records
6. Repatriating from NRO without 15CA/CB compliance
7. Using debit card heavily abroad (high forex markup) instead of forex card

REPATRIATION OF SALE PROCEEDS OF INHERITED PROPERTY
- USD 1 million / year out of NRO
- Plus Form 15CA / 15CB
- Long-Term Capital Gains tax paid; can use Section 54EC bonds for exemption

REPATRIATION ON DEATH OF NRI
- Death certificate + succession certificate or will
- Bank processes claim
- Proceeds repatriable to legal heir abroad (if heir is also NRI) subject to USD 1 million NRO cap per year
- Within Indian inheritance laws

REGULATORY UPDATES — STAY INFORMED
- RBI Master Direction on FEMA — updated periodically
- Income Tax circulars on TCS, TDS rates
- Bank's NRI banking circulars for product changes
- RBI FAQs for NRIs

REMITTANCE RECEIPT DOCUMENTATION (For Indian tax purposes)
- FIRA (Foreign Inward Remittance Advice / Certificate): For tax filing, proving the remittance is foreign-source
- SWIFT MT103 message: Detailed remittance record
- Bank statement: Shows credit
- These help establish source of funds for tax inquiry / audit

KEY TAKEAWAYS
1. Compare rates and fees across remittance methods — Wise / Remitly often cheapest
2. Use NRE for tax-free interest; NRO for Indian income
3. FCNR for foreign currency time deposit (hedge against INR fall)
4. LRS cap of USD 2,50,000 for residents
5. TCS 20% applies on LRS above ₹7 lakh (some exceptions)
6. Form 15CA / 15CB mandatory for outward NRO remittance
7. Claim DTAA benefits with TRC + Form 10F
8. Use Forex card for international travel
9. Keep FIRA / transaction records for tax filings
10. Plan large remittances around FY (April-March) — TCS resets annually

Rates and rules may change, verify with bank / RBI / Income Tax for current FX policies, TCS rates, and DTAA terms.
