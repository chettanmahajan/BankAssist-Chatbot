NRI ACCOUNTS — NRE, NRO, FCNR(B), DIFFERENCES, TAXATION

OVERVIEW
Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) cannot maintain regular savings/current accounts in India under FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999). They must use specialised NRI accounts. These accounts are designed to:
- Receive foreign income / remittance from abroad
- Manage Indian income (rent, dividends, pension)
- Invest in India
- Send money home and back abroad

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) regulates NRI accounts under FEMA and various Master Directions. The three primary NRI account types are NRE, NRO, and FCNR(B). All figures are indicative; rates may change, verify with bank.

WHO IS AN NRI?
Defined under FEMA based on residency status (not citizenship):
- Indian citizens residing abroad for employment, business, or any other purpose indicating an indefinite period of stay
- Indian citizens on Indian government / UN deputation outside India
- Students pursuing higher studies abroad (subject to specific provisions)

Definition under Income Tax Act is slightly different (based on days of stay in India during a financial year — typically less than 182 days qualifies as non-resident).

DOCUMENTS TO ESTABLISH NRI STATUS:
- Valid passport (with all relevant pages)
- Valid work permit, employment visa, residence permit, business visa
- For PIO/OCI: PIO card or OCI card + foreign passport
- Overseas address proof (utility bill, residence permit, lease agreement)
- Indian address proof (optional but recommended)
- PAN or Form 60
- Photographs

NRE ACCOUNT — NON-RESIDENT EXTERNAL (RUPEE) ACCOUNT

Currency: Indian Rupees (INR)

Purpose: To park foreign earnings / income earned outside India, converted to INR

Operation:
- Can be Savings, Current, or Term Deposit (FD)
- Joint operation: Allowed with another NRI (Either or Survivor / Anyone or Survivor)
- Joint with Resident Indian: Allowed since RBI relaxation 2011 (former-or-survivor basis)

Source of Funds:
- Inward remittance from abroad (foreign currency converted to INR at the bank)
- Transfer from another NRE / FCNR account
- Income earned abroad (salary, business, dividends from foreign sources)
- Cannot deposit:
  - Indian income (must go to NRO)
  - Cash deposits in India (unless personal cheques from own NRO drawn out)
  - Local Indian transactions credited

Repatriation:
- Principal and interest FULLY repatriable (can be transferred back abroad in foreign currency)
- No upper limit
- Useful for those expecting to need funds outside India

Tax Treatment:
- Interest earned: FULLY TAX-FREE in India
- TDS: Not deducted
- Income from NRE deposits is exempt under Section 10(4)(ii) of Income Tax Act for NRIs

Interest Rates (FY 2024-25 indicative):
- NRE Savings: 2.7%-4% p.a. (varies by bank)
- NRE FD (1-3 year): 6.6%-7.25%
- NRE FD (3-5 year): 6.5%-7.0%
- Rates similar to resident savings/FD; sometimes slightly lower due to repatriability premium

Card / Banking Features:
- ATM/Debit card available, can be used internationally
- Net banking and mobile banking
- Cheque book
- Standing instructions

Conversion on Return:
- When account holder returns to India for permanent settlement, NRE account is re-designated as Resident savings account
- Interest from re-designation onwards becomes taxable

NRO ACCOUNT — NON-RESIDENT ORDINARY (RUPEE) ACCOUNT

Currency: Indian Rupees (INR)

Purpose: To manage Indian-source income while abroad

Operation:
- Savings, Current, or Fixed Deposit
- Joint with another NRI or Resident Indian: Allowed
- When NRI status begins, the existing resident savings account is mandatorily re-designated as NRO

Source of Funds:
- Indian-source income: Rent, dividends, interest, pension, capital gains, share of profit from Indian businesses
- Cash deposits from Indian sources
- Inward remittance from abroad (also permitted, though usually goes to NRE for tax-free interest)
- Transfer from NRO of self or close relative (with specifications)

Repatriation:
- Limited to USD 1 million per financial year (April-March) — after applicable taxes
- Both current income and capital gains can be remitted within this limit
- Requires Form 15CA and 15CB (CA certification for tax compliance) for remittances

Tax Treatment:
- Interest earned: Fully taxable as per Indian slab rates
- TDS: Deducted at 30% (plus surcharge and cess) — even on small amounts
- Tax-free threshold and slabs apply at filing; refund possible if TDS exceeds tax liability
- DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement): If beneficial, lower rate (typically 10%-15% for interest) applies on submission of TRC (Tax Residency Certificate) and Form 10F

Interest Rates (FY 2024-25 indicative):
- NRO Savings: 2.7%-4% p.a.
- NRO FD: Similar to NRE FD

Card / Banking Features:
- Debit card, net banking, cheque book
- Card can be used in India and abroad

When to Use NRO:
- Receiving rent from Indian property
- Dividends from Indian shares
- Pension from Indian employer
- Sale proceeds of Indian property
- Income from any Indian business

When NRI returns:
- NRO is re-designated as resident savings account
- Tax treatment becomes resident-applicable

FCNR(B) — FOREIGN CURRENCY NON-RESIDENT (BANK) ACCOUNT

Currency: Major foreign currencies — USD, GBP, EUR, JPY, CAD, AUD, SGD, HKD, CHF (depends on bank)

Purpose: Term deposit in foreign currency to protect against rupee depreciation

Operation:
- Only Term Deposit (no savings or current)
- Minimum tenure: 1 year
- Maximum tenure: 5 years (some banks allow longer)
- Single or joint with another NRI

Source of Funds:
- Inward remittance from abroad in foreign currency
- Transfer from existing FCNR / NRE
- Cannot fund from NRO

Repatriation:
- Principal and interest FULLY repatriable
- Useful for those wanting to retain foreign currency assets

Tax Treatment:
- Interest: FULLY TAX-FREE in India (Section 10(15)(iv)(fa))
- TDS: Not deducted
- Excellent for tax-conscious NRIs

Interest Rates (FY 2024-25 indicative, varies by currency and tenure):
- USD: 5.0%-5.7% for 1-3 year FCNR (FY24-25 indicative — varies with LIBOR/SOFR replacements and bank policy)
- GBP: 4.5%-5.5%
- EUR: 3.5%-4.5%
- JPY: 0%-1%
- AUD: 4.5%-5.5%
- CAD: 4.0%-5.0%

Rates change with international interest rate environment.

Premature Withdrawal:
- Allowed but no interest paid if withdrawn within 12 months (FCNR rule)
- After 12 months: Interest at applicable rate minus penalty

Risk Factors:
- Currency conversion at maturity (if repatriating to country with different currency)
- LIBOR replacement (now SOFR for USD) volatility

WHEN TO USE WHICH ACCOUNT — DECISION FRAMEWORK

Use NRE when:
- You earn abroad and want to invest in India
- Need full repatriability
- Want tax-free interest
- Will primarily use funds in India (INR-denominated)

Use NRO when:
- You have ongoing Indian-source income (rent, dividends, pension)
- Need to receive payments in India
- Want to manage existing Indian financial commitments

Use FCNR when:
- You earn abroad and want to retain foreign currency exposure
- Need protection against INR depreciation
- Want tax-free interest
- Have a 1-5 year horizon for funds before repatriation

Many NRIs maintain all three:
- NRE for foreign earnings → INR investment in India
- NRO for Indian income management
- FCNR for foreign currency time deposits

COMPARISON TABLE — NRE vs NRO vs FCNR(B)

| Feature | NRE | NRO | FCNR(B) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Currency | INR | INR | Foreign (USD, GBP, EUR, etc.) |
| Source of funds | Abroad income | Indian income | Abroad income |
| Repatriation | Full | Up to USD 1M/year | Full |
| Tax on interest | Tax-free | Taxable, 30% TDS | Tax-free |
| Account types | Savings, Current, FD | Savings, Current, FD | FD only |
| Joint with Resident | Yes (former or survivor) | Yes | No |
| Minimum tenure (FD) | 1 year typically | 7 days | 1 year |

ACCOUNT OPENING — STEP BY STEP

For NRIs already abroad:
1. Choose bank with strong NRI offering (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, BoB, Kotak, Yes, Federal Bank, IDBI, IDFC FIRST)
2. Download account opening form from bank's website
3. Fill and self-attest
4. Get documents attested by:
   - Indian Embassy / Consulate, OR
   - Notary Public abroad, OR
   - Banker abroad (with banker's stamp), OR
   - Indian Banker (if you visit branch during India trip)
5. Submit by courier or in person at branch on next India visit
6. Open via Video KYC — some banks (HDFC, ICICI, Axis, SBI) now support
7. Initial funding via inward remittance

Time: 7-15 working days at branch; faster with V-KYC.

For NRIs visiting India:
1. Walk in to any branch with original passport, visa, address proof
2. Fill AOF
3. Open account same day; usually active within 24 hours
4. Inward remittance to fund account

DEBIT CARDS AND BANKING FEATURES
- Free international debit card (typically Visa Platinum / RuPay Global)
- Tap-to-pay, contactless
- Withdrawal at international ATMs (with forex markup)
- POS purchases internationally
- Net banking and mobile banking full-featured
- Cheque book on request
- Locker facility

INVESTMENTS PERMITTED FOR NRIs

Permitted:
- Equity shares (PIS — Portfolio Investment Scheme, regulated by RBI)
- Mutual funds (subject to AMC's NRI acceptance)
- Bonds, NCDs (Non-Convertible Debentures)
- Bank FDs (NRE/NRO/FCNR)
- Government securities (G-Sec)
- Real estate (residential / commercial — not agricultural/farmhouse land/plantation)
- Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)
- NPS (National Pension System)

Not Permitted:
- Agricultural land, farmhouse, plantation property
- PPF (existing PPF before NRI status can continue; no fresh PPF)
- Post Office Schemes (except some — check current rules)
- Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS)

CAPITAL GAINS TAX FOR NRIs

Equity Mutual Funds / Stocks:
- STCG (held < 12 months): 20% (raised in Budget 2024)
- LTCG (held > 12 months): 12.5% on gains exceeding ₹1.25 lakh

Debt Mutual Funds (purchased after April 2023): Slab rate

Property:
- STCG (held < 24 months): Slab rate (often 30%+ for high-income NRIs)
- LTCG (held > 24 months): 20% with indexation (or 12.5% without indexation, per Budget 2024 amendment — NRIs can choose either method)
- TDS on property sale: 20% LTCG, 30% STCG (plus surcharge/cess) deducted by buyer; refund possible if tax liability is lower
- LTCG exemption under Section 54 (reinvest in property) or 54F (other capital asset) or 54EC (bonds) available to NRIs

DTAA (DOUBLE TAXATION AVOIDANCE AGREEMENT)
India has DTAA with 80+ countries (USA, UK, UAE, Singapore, Australia, Canada, Saudi Arabia, etc.). Benefits:
- Tax credit in country of residence for tax paid in India
- Reduced TDS rates on certain incomes (interest, royalty, fees for technical services)
- Avoidance of double taxation
- Submit TRC (Tax Residency Certificate from foreign country) + Form 10F + self-declaration to claim DTAA benefits with Indian bank/payer

INWARD REMITTANCE TO INDIA — METHODS
1. Wire transfer (SWIFT): Most common, 1-3 days, fee USD 10-50 from foreign bank + recipient bank
2. Online remittance services: Wise, Remitly, Western Union, MoneyGram, Xoom — competitive rates and lower fees
3. RBI-approved Money Transfer Operators (MTOs)
4. Foreign currency demand draft sent to Indian bank
5. Bank-specific remittance platforms: SBI ExpressRemit, HDFC RemitNow, ICICI Money2India, Axis RemitMoney, Kotak Click2Remit
6. Net banking from foreign bank account to NRE/NRO of Indian bank

OUTWARD REMITTANCE FROM INDIA (FOR NRI)
- From NRE: No limit, freely repatriable
- From NRO: USD 1 million per FY (April-March), after tax clearance
- Form 15CA + 15CB (CA certificate) required for outward remittance from NRO (some exceptions for amount thresholds)

KYC RE-VERIFICATION ON RESIDENCY CHANGE
- When NRI returns to India permanently:
  - NRE / NRO → Resident savings account (re-designation)
  - FCNR can continue till maturity, then either repatriate or convert to RFC (Resident Foreign Currency)
- Inform bank within reasonable time
- Tax treatment changes accordingly

RFC ACCOUNT — RESIDENT FOREIGN CURRENCY
- For returning Indians (who were NRIs for at least 1 year)
- Maintain foreign currency in India
- Useful during transition period
- Interest tax-treatment depends on residency status

PIO / OCI BANKING
PIO (Persons of Indian Origin) and OCI (Overseas Citizens of India) cards holders are treated as NRIs for banking purposes (FEMA definition). Same account types apply.

NRI MUTUAL FUND INVESTMENT
- USD-based NRIs (US, Canada): Restricted access to MF due to FATCA reporting; only select AMCs (e.g., L&T, ICICI, Mirae, Sundaram, Aditya Birla) accept
- Other NRIs: Most AMCs accept
- KYC required (NRI status)
- Repatriable / Non-repatriable basis (depends on source — NRE for repatriable, NRO for non-repatriable)

KEY ADVANTAGES OF NRI ACCOUNTS
1. Access to India's banking infrastructure
2. Tax-free interest on NRE / FCNR
3. Foreign currency hedge via FCNR
4. Smooth Indian income management via NRO
5. Investment in India (equity, MF, FD, property)
6. Repatriation flexibility

COMMON MISTAKES BY NRIs
1. Not converting savings account to NRO on becoming NRI (technical FEMA violation)
2. Depositing Indian income to NRE (not allowed)
3. Not submitting Form 15CA/CB for outward remittance
4. Not claiming DTAA benefits (paying more tax than required)
5. Ignoring tax filing in India for NRO interest
6. Not nominating a relative for NRI accounts
7. Letting NRE FDs auto-renew at lower rates without comparison
8. Not informing bank when returning to India

DOORSTEP BANKING FOR NRIs
- Document collection during India visits
- Video KYC for new accounts
- Digital signing of documents
- Email / chat-based support

For specific bank's NRI offerings and detailed terms, refer to the bank's NRI banking page. Rates and rules may change, verify with bank / RBI.
