CREDIT CARD BASICS — TYPES, ELIGIBILITY, FEES, REWARDS

WHAT IS A CREDIT CARD?
A credit card is a payment instrument issued by a bank that allows the cardholder to borrow funds up to a pre-approved credit limit to pay for goods, services, and bills. Unlike a debit card (which uses your own balance), a credit card uses the bank's money, which must be repaid within a billing cycle (usually 30 days). If the full statement balance is repaid by the due date, no interest is charged — making credit cards effectively a 20-50 day interest-free loan. If only the minimum amount is paid, the unpaid balance attracts interest at a high rate (36%-48% per annum). Credit cards in India are regulated by RBI under the Master Direction on Credit Card and Debit Card – Issuance and Conduct Directions, 2022. Rates and fees mentioned are indicative; rates may change, verify with bank.

CREDIT CARD TYPES BY TIER

1. SILVER / CLASSIC / BASIC: Entry-level card for first-time users or low-income segment.
- Credit limit: ₹15,000 to ₹1 lakh
- Annual fee: ₹0 (lifetime free) to ₹500
- Eligibility: Annual income ₹2 lakh+
- Rewards: Basic — 0.5%-1% cashback or reward points
- Examples: SBI SimplyClick, HDFC MoneyBack, Axis MyZone

2. GOLD: Mid-tier card with better rewards and limits.
- Credit limit: ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakh
- Annual fee: ₹500 to ₹1,500
- Eligibility: Annual income ₹3 lakh+
- Rewards: 1%-2% reward rate, accelerated rewards on select categories (dining, fuel, online)
- Lounge access: 1-2 free domestic airport lounge visits per quarter
- Examples: HDFC Regalia First, ICICI Coral, Axis Ace

3. PLATINUM: Premium card with enhanced privileges.
- Credit limit: ₹3 lakh to ₹15 lakh
- Annual fee: ₹1,500 to ₹5,000 (often waived on spending milestones)
- Eligibility: Annual income ₹6 lakh+
- Rewards: 2%-5% on selected categories, milestone benefits, golf, dining
- Lounge access: 8-16 domestic + 4-8 international per year
- Examples: HDFC Regalia, SBI Prime, ICICI Sapphiro, Axis Magnus

4. SIGNATURE: High-end cards for affluent customers.
- Credit limit: ₹5 lakh to ₹25 lakh
- Annual fee: ₹3,000 to ₹10,000
- Eligibility: Annual income ₹10-15 lakh+
- Rewards: Higher accelerated points, concierge service, premium dining
- Examples: HDFC Diners Privilege, ICICI Emeralde Private Metal (premium variant), Yes Marquee

5. INFINITE / WORLD ELITE: Super-premium cards for HNIs.
- Credit limit: ₹10 lakh to ₹50 lakh+ (or no preset limit)
- Annual fee: ₹10,000 to ₹50,000 (some waivable)
- Eligibility: Annual income ₹25 lakh+ / private banking customers
- Benefits: Unlimited international lounge access (Priority Pass), golf, concierge, butler services, complimentary nights at luxury hotels, premium insurance
- Examples: HDFC Infinia, Axis Magnus, ICICI Emeralde Private Metal, SBI Aurum, Diners Club Black

SPECIAL CATEGORY CARDS

Co-branded cards: Issued in partnership with airlines, retailers, fuel companies, e-commerce. Accelerated rewards in the partner's ecosystem.
- Air Miles: Vistara, Air India SBI Signature, IndiGo HDFC
- Retail: Amazon Pay ICICI, Flipkart Axis, Swiggy HDFC, Tata Neu HDFC
- Fuel: BPCL SBI Octane, IndianOil Citi (now discontinued), HPCL IDFC

Business / Corporate cards: For company expenses, separate from personal limits.

Secured credit cards: Issued against a Fixed Deposit (typically 80%-100% of FD as credit limit). For applicants with low/no credit history. Examples: SBI Unnati, ICICI Coral against FD, Axis Insta Easy.

Add-on cards: Supplementary cards for family members (spouse, parents, children 18+) on the same account. Usage adds to primary cardholder's bill. Often free or low fee.

Forex/Travel cards: Loaded with foreign currency for international travel. Lower forex markup than regular cards.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
- Age: 21 to 65 years (some entry-level cards 18+)
- Income: ₹2 lakh+ p.a. minimum for entry-level; ₹6 lakh+ for premium; ₹15 lakh+ for super-premium
- Employment: Salaried or self-employed with stable income
- Credit Score (CIBIL): 700+ recommended; 750+ for premium cards; first-time applicants without score may qualify for secured cards
- KYC: PAN mandatory, Aadhaar for e-KYC
- Address: Indian residence

JOINING AND ANNUAL FEES
- Joining fee: One-time fee for issuing the card. Range ₹0 to ₹10,000. Often waived under promotional offers.
- Annual fee: Charged each year (usually at first anniversary onwards). Range ₹0 (lifetime free LTF) to ₹50,000 (super-premium).
- Annual fee waiver: Most cards waive the annual fee if a minimum spend threshold is met in the previous year (e.g., spend ₹1.5 lakh on HDFC Regalia to get next year's fee waived).
- GST 18% is added on all fees.

CREDIT LIMIT — HOW IT'S DECIDED
The bank's underwriting team determines the credit limit based on:
- Stated and verified income (last 3 months' salary slips, Form 16, ITR)
- Existing credit obligations (other credit cards, loans) — CIBIL report check
- Employer category (premium employers get higher limits)
- Banking relationship (existing accounts, balances, salary credit)
- Repayment behaviour on previous credit
Limit can be enhanced after 6-12 months of regular usage and on-time payments. You can also request a limit reduction if you want tighter spending control.

REWARDS AND CASHBACK
Most credit cards offer reward points (1 point = ₹0.20-₹0.50 typically) or cashback on every transaction:
- Base rate: 0.5%-2% on all spends
- Accelerated categories: 5%-10% on partner merchants, online shopping, dining, fuel
- Milestones: Bonus points/vouchers for crossing quarterly/annual spend levels
- Reward redemption: Statement credit, vouchers (Amazon, Flipkart), products from rewards catalogue, AirMiles, travel bookings

Restrictions on rewards:
- Rent payments, fuel, wallet loads, government transactions, utilities often excluded or capped
- Smart bankers know to read the fine print before optimising spend categories
- Reward points typically expire 2-3 years after earning if unused

CASHBACK CARDS
Some cards give direct cashback instead of points:
- Axis Ace: 5% on Google Pay bill payments, 4% on Swiggy/Zomato, 2% on rest
- SBI Cashback Card: 5% online (no merchant restriction), 1% offline
- HDFC Millennia: 5% on partner brands, 1% on rest
- Cashback is usually auto-credited as a statement adjustment monthly or quarterly.

LIFETIME FREE (LTF) CARDS
Cards with no joining or annual fee throughout the life of the card. Excellent for users who don't want to track spend thresholds. Common LTF cards: HDFC MoneyBack+ (LTF on milestone), Amazon Pay ICICI (LTF for Prime users), Axis Flipkart (LTF for select users), IDFC FIRST cards (multiple LTF options), Yes Prosperity, ICICI Platinum Chip.

CONTACTLESS PAYMENTS
All new Indian credit cards support contactless tap-to-pay via NFC. Transactions up to ₹5,000 work without PIN; above ₹5,000 requires PIN. Useful for quick small-ticket payments — coffee, transport, groceries.

EMI ON CREDIT CARDS
Most banks allow you to convert a credit card transaction (typically above ₹3,000-₹10,000) into EMI:
- Tenure 3 to 24 months
- Interest rate 11%-18% p.a. (lower than revolving credit rate)
- Processing fee: 1%-3% of transaction amount or ₹150-₹500
- No-cost EMI on partner merchants (Amazon, Flipkart, Croma) — interest is discounted upfront so net cost equals cash price (but RBI mandates clear disclosure)

BILL PAYMENT
Bills can be paid via:
- Auto-debit (NACH mandate)
- UPI to the card
- IMPS / NEFT
- Net banking
- Cheque
- Cash at branch (charges apply ₹50-₹250)
Set auto-debit for minimum amount due (MAD) to avoid late fees, and pay the rest manually as cash flow permits. Auto-debit for total amount is safest for those with consistent income.

STATEMENT GENERATION AND DUE DATE
- Billing cycle: Typically 25-30 days. Statement date is fixed for your card.
- Due date: Usually 18-25 days after statement date. Total cycle = up to 50 days of interest-free credit.
- Minimum amount due (MAD): 5% of statement balance + interest + fees + over-limit + EMI portion. Always at least pay MAD.

CREDIT CARD VS. DEBIT CARD
| Feature | Credit Card | Debit Card |
|---|---|---|
| Funds | Bank's money | Your own |
| Interest | If unpaid post due date | None |
| Credit Score Impact | Yes | No |
| Rewards | Higher rewards | Limited |
| Fraud Protection | Strong (RBI zero liability) | Strong |
| ATM Cash | Cash advance fee + interest from day 1 | Standard |

KEY ADVANTAGES OF CREDIT CARDS
1. Builds credit history and CIBIL score
2. Interest-free borrowing if repaid on time
3. Rewards and cashbacks effectively give discounts
4. Strong fraud protection
5. Useful for online and international transactions
6. EMI conversion for big purchases
7. Lounge access and travel benefits

WHEN NOT TO USE A CREDIT CARD
1. Cash withdrawal at ATM (immediate interest + transaction fee)
2. Wallet loads beyond reward caps
3. If you cannot pay in full and habitually carry balance (36%-48% interest)
4. For everyday small purchases if you tend to overspend
