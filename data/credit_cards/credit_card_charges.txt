CREDIT CARD CHARGES — INTEREST, FEES, AND COSTS EXPLAINED

OVERVIEW
Credit cards are convenient but expensive when used carelessly. This document explains every charge a cardholder may incur. RBI's Master Direction on Credit Card and Debit Card – Issuance and Conduct Directions, 2022, mandates clear disclosure of all charges in the Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC) document and on the card website. All figures below are indicative as of FY 2024-25; rates may change, verify with bank for your specific card.

INTEREST RATE (FINANCE CHARGE)
This is the single largest cost on a credit card. When you don't pay the full statement balance by the due date, interest is charged on the unpaid balance from the date of each transaction (retrospectively) until repayment. Indian credit card interest rates:
- Standard range: 3.0% to 4.0% per month, i.e., 36% to 48% per annum
- Premium/super-premium cards: 3.10%-3.5% per month
- Entry-level cards: 3.5%-4% per month
- Some new fintech cards: 3.0% per month (lower end)

Interest is compounded monthly — so the effective annual rate is even higher than the simple multiplication. GST 18% is applicable on the interest amount.

CRUCIAL DETAIL — REVERSAL OF INTEREST-FREE CREDIT
If you pay even ₹1 less than the total amount due by the due date:
- The interest-free credit period is lost for the entire billing cycle
- Interest is charged retrospectively on each transaction from its transaction date
- Any new purchases until full repayment also accrue interest from day 1
- This is why partial payments are often expensive — pay in full or expect substantial finance charges

Example: Statement balance ₹50,000 due on the 25th. You pay ₹49,000 on time and ₹1,000 a week later. Interest is calculated on ₹50,000 (and not just ₹1,000) from each transaction date, often resulting in ₹1,500-₹2,500 interest charge despite only owing ₹1,000.

MINIMUM AMOUNT DUE (MAD)
The MAD is typically 5% of the statement outstanding plus any over-limit, EMI amount, fees, and full interest. Paying only the MAD:
- Avoids the late payment penalty
- Keeps the account regular (no CIBIL hit)
- BUT does NOT avoid interest charges — interest is levied on full outstanding from each transaction date

RBI recently mandated disclosure on credit card statements warning that paying only MAD will significantly extend repayment and increase interest cost — to discourage minimum-payment habit.

LATE PAYMENT FEE
If you fail to pay even the MAD by the due date, a late payment fee is charged. Recent RBI-aligned slab (banks may differ slightly):
- Statement balance ₹0 to ₹500: Nil
- ₹501 to ₹5,000: ₹500
- ₹5,001 to ₹10,000: ₹600 to ₹750
- ₹10,001 to ₹25,000: ₹800 to ₹950
- ₹25,001 to ₹50,000: ₹1,100 to ₹1,200
- Above ₹50,000: ₹1,300 (capped per RBI guidance)
GST 18% applicable on late fee. Banks must report 30+ day delay to CIBIL as Days Past Due, hurting credit score.

OVER-LIMIT FEE
If you exceed your sanctioned credit limit (some cards permit this with prior consent), you are charged:
- Flat ₹500 to ₹600 per cycle, or 2.5% of over-limit amount, whichever is higher
Some cards do not permit over-limit usage at all — transaction will be declined.

CASH ADVANCE FEE (CASH WITHDRAWAL FROM ATM USING CREDIT CARD)
Cash withdrawal from ATM via credit card is the most expensive use:
- Fee: 2.5% to 3.5% of cash withdrawn, minimum ₹500
- Interest: From the date of withdrawal at full revolving rate (36%-48% p.a.) — NO interest-free period
- Daily limit: Usually 20%-40% of credit limit, per card terms
- Always avoid cash advances unless absolute emergency

FOREIGN CURRENCY MARKUP / FOREX FEE
When you use your credit card for transactions in foreign currency (international travel, foreign websites):
- Forex markup: 1.5% to 3.5% of transaction amount
- Forex card and select premium cards have lower 0%-2% markup
- DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion) at merchant: Always decline — pay in local currency
- GST 18% applicable on the markup fee

Examples:
- HDFC Regalia: 2% markup
- ICICI Sapphiro: 1.99%
- Axis Magnus: 2%
- Forex-friendly cards: HDFC InfiniaMetal (2%), IDFC Wow (0%), Niyo Global (debit, but worth mention)
- Niyo Forex Plus card: 0%-1% markup with locked-in rates

JOINING FEE AND ANNUAL FEE
- Joining fee: One-time at issuance — ₹0 to ₹10,000
- Annual fee: Each year from second year — ₹0 (lifetime free) to ₹50,000
- Most cards offer annual fee waiver on meeting a spend threshold (e.g., ₹1.5 lakh in previous year)
- GST 18% added

ADD-ON CARD FEE
Add-on (supplementary) cards for family members may have a small annual fee (₹250-₹1,500) or be free, depending on the card. Premium cards typically offer free add-ons.

REWARD REDEMPTION FEE
- Catalogue redemption: ₹99 per redemption (most banks)
- Statement credit / cashback redemption: Free
- AirMiles transfer: ₹99-₹500

EMI CONVERSION CHARGES
Converting a purchase to EMI on credit card:
- Interest: 11%-18% p.a. (varies by bank, tenure, and customer profile)
- Processing fee: 1%-3% of transaction or ₹99-₹500 flat
- Foreclosure of EMI: 3% of remaining principal (some banks free)
- No-cost EMI: Available on partner merchants — interest is offset by merchant discount upfront

DEMAND DRAFT, BALANCE TRANSFER, AND OTHER FEES
- Balance transfer fee: 1.5%-3% of transferred amount (one-time)
- Demand draft against credit card: 1.5%-3% of amount + fee
- Statement reprint (physical): ₹100 per cycle
- Duplicate physical statement after 3 months: ₹150
- PIN regeneration: ₹50-₹100
- Replacement card: ₹100-₹500 (free in case of damage on first request)
- Card upgrade fee: Usually nil
- Settlement/closure: Generally free if balance is nil

CHEQUE BOUNCE / NACH BOUNCE FEE
If your auto-debit instruction fails due to insufficient balance in linked bank account:
- Bounce charge ₹500-₹750 + GST
- Plus late payment fee for that cycle
- Penal interest on the unpaid amount

GST ON ALL FEES AND INTEREST
Goods and Services Tax at 18% is applicable on all credit card fees, interest, and service charges. This is a flat addition to whatever charge the bank levies. So a ₹500 late fee actually costs ₹590, and ₹2,000 interest actually costs ₹2,360.

CHARGES YOU SHOULD NEVER PAY (KNOW YOUR RIGHTS)
- Forced sale of insurance, gold coin, mutual fund along with credit card: NOT allowed by RBI
- Unsolicited issue of credit card without consent: Banned by RBI 2022 Master Direction
- Charges not disclosed in MITC: Disputable
- Charges levied for services you didn't avail: Disputable

OBSCURE CHARGES TO WATCH FOR
- Rent payment fee: Some cards charge 1% on rent transactions (paid via apps like CRED Pay, NoBroker) up to ₹1,500 — to curb cycling
- Wallet load fee: Most cards charge 1%-2% on Paytm/MobiKwik wallet loads
- Fuel transaction fee: 1% surcharge on fuel transactions, capped at ₹150-₹200 — often waived if surcharge waiver feature is part of the card
- Government transactions: Some cards exclude tax payments from rewards or charge 1%
- Educational fees via third-party platforms: Some cards levy fee on third-party education payment apps

PROTECTION ON DISPUTED TRANSACTIONS
RBI mandates 30-day investigation for disputed transactions. If the transaction is found unauthorised or invalid, the bank must reverse the charge plus pro-rated interest, fees, and any consequential late fees.

ZERO LIABILITY PROTECTION
For unauthorised electronic transactions reported within 3 working days of receiving communication, the customer's liability is zero (subject to RBI Limited Liability framework). After 4-7 days, liability is capped at ₹10,000-₹25,000; beyond 7 days, customer may bear full loss. Always report card loss or unauthorised transaction immediately to the bank's 24×7 helpline or via app.

HOW INTEREST IS CALCULATED — EXAMPLE
Suppose your billing cycle is 1st-30th. Statement date: 30th. Due date: 18th of next month.
- 5 Jan: Purchase ₹10,000
- 20 Jan: Purchase ₹5,000
- 30 Jan: Statement generated, total ₹15,000
- 18 Feb: Due date — you pay only ₹3,000

Interest is now charged from each transaction date:
- ₹10,000 from 5 Jan to 18 Feb: ~44 days at ~3.5% per month
- ₹5,000 from 20 Jan to 18 Feb: ~29 days at ~3.5% per month
- Plus interest on the unpaid ₹12,000 from 19 Feb until next payment
This easily adds ₹600-₹900 of interest charges plus GST 18%.

TIPS TO AVOID UNNECESSARY CHARGES
1. Set auto-debit for total statement amount (not just MAD)
2. Pay multiple times during the cycle if needed — drop the balance to zero
3. Never withdraw cash from credit card ATM
4. Don't use the card for forex transactions if a forex card is available
5. Don't load wallets or buy gold/cryptocurrency on credit card
6. Avoid carrying over balances — if cash flow is tight, take a personal loan at lower interest
7. Read MITC for your specific card
8. Use the bank's app to track spends in real-time and set alerts

Rates may change, verify with bank — the MITC and Schedule of Charges on the bank website are the authoritative source for your specific card and date.
