CREDIT CARD FEATURES — EMI, BALANCE TRANSFER, LOUNGE ACCESS, ADD-ONS, SURCHARGE WAIVERS

EMI CONVERSION
Credit card EMI is a facility that converts a large transaction into manageable monthly installments. Available on most major banks for transactions above a threshold (typically ₹3,000 to ₹10,000). Features:
- Tenure options: 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months (some banks offer up to 36 months for big-ticket items)
- Interest rate: 11%-18% per annum (lower than the 36%-48% revolving credit rate)
- Processing fee: 1%-3% of transaction amount, or ₹99-₹500 flat
- GST 18% on interest and processing fee
- Foreclosure: 3% of outstanding principal (banks vary; some offer free foreclosure)

How EMI Conversion Works:
1. After a purchase, log into your bank's app/portal or call customer care
2. Choose the transaction and select EMI tenure
3. The transaction is converted within 7-10 business days
4. The original purchase amount + processing fee block the same credit limit until repaid
5. Each EMI shows up in your monthly statement; you must continue paying that amount along with new spends

NO-COST EMI / NO-INTEREST EMI
Offered on partner e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, Croma, Reliance Digital) and select merchants. The interest amount is offset by a discount given by the merchant, so net out-of-pocket equals MRP. RBI has mandated transparent disclosure — the "discount" is shown in your statement so you can see the breakup. Conditions:
- Available only on partner platforms during eligible promotions
- Excluded from reward points/cashback on most cards
- Processing fee may still apply (₹99-₹199)

BALANCE TRANSFER (BT) ON CREDIT CARDS
Move outstanding balance from one credit card (typically high interest) to another card at a promotional rate to save interest. Features:
- Promotional rate: 0% to 1% per month for 3-6 months (vs. 3%-4% revolving)
- BT processing fee: 1.5%-3% of transferred amount
- Tenure: 3 to 12 months typically
- Eligibility: Existing credit card customers with regular payment history
Useful only if you have a concrete plan to repay during the low-interest period. Failure to repay reverts to the standard high rate.

LOUNGE ACCESS
Premium and super-premium credit cards offer complimentary access to domestic and international airport lounges:

Domestic Lounges:
- Entry-level cards: 1-2 visits per quarter
- Premium cards (HDFC Regalia, ICICI Sapphiro, SBI Prime): 4 visits per quarter, total 16/year
- Super-premium (HDFC Infinia, Diners Black, Axis Magnus): 8-12+ visits per quarter or unlimited

International Lounges (via Priority Pass or DreamFolks):
- Premium cards: 4-8 visits per year
- Super-premium: Unlimited visits with primary + guest access

Eligibility nuances:
- Some cards require minimum spend per quarter to unlock lounge access
- Lounge entry requires physical or digital lounge access voucher / card swipe at lounge desk
- Indian banks often link with Priority Pass (Visa/Mastercard) or DreamFolks (a domestic lounge aggregator) for access

GOLF PRIVILEGES
Premium cards (HDFC Infinia, Diners Black, Axis Reserve) offer:
- Free golf games at participating courses
- Free golf lessons (typically 1-4 per year)
- Discounted green fees at premium courses
Used mainly by leisure golfers; may not benefit non-golf customers.

CONCIERGE SERVICES
Premium cards offer 24×7 concierge service via toll-free number or app:
- Travel bookings: Flights, hotels, car rentals
- Restaurant reservations at premium establishments
- Event ticketing
- Gift sourcing
- Document courier and emergency assistance
Concierge service is typically free, but the actual cost of any service booked is borne by the customer.

DINING PROGRAMS
Most premium cards integrate with dining programs:
- ICICI Culinary Treats: 15%-25% off at partner restaurants
- HDFC Good Food Trail: 15%-20% off
- SBI Vishesh: similar discounts
- Visa Dining Program / Mastercard Priceless Cities: city-specific dining offers
- Diners Club Restaurant Network: Special menus at premium restaurants

INSURANCE COVERS BUNDLED WITH CARDS
Many premium cards offer complimentary insurance:
- Air accident insurance: ₹50 lakh to ₹2.5 crore
- Lost baggage cover: ₹50,000 to ₹2 lakh
- Travel delay cover: ₹10,000-₹50,000
- Lost card liability cover: Up to ₹3 lakh
- Purchase protection: 30-180 days cover on items bought
- Fraud protection / cyber risk cover
Note: Coverage triggers usually require booking the eligible travel on the same credit card. Read the policy wording.

ADD-ON CARDS (SUPPLEMENTARY CARDS)
Issued to family members (spouse, parents, siblings, children 18+):
- Up to 5 add-on cards per primary cardholder
- All spending on add-on shows on primary's statement
- Add-on cards share the primary's credit limit
- Often free or nominal fee
- Useful for managing family finances, gifting partner buying power, or earning more rewards

FUEL SURCHARGE WAIVER
Indian credit cards charge a 1% fuel surcharge on petrol/diesel transactions. Most cards waive this surcharge if certain conditions are met:
- Transaction value typically ₹400 to ₹5,000
- Waiver capped at ₹100-₹250 per statement cycle
- Examples:
  - HDFC Millennia: 1% fuel surcharge waiver up to ₹250 per cycle
  - Axis Vistara: Waiver on transactions ₹400-₹4,000, capped ₹400 per cycle
  - SBI Octane: Waiver + accelerated rewards on fuel
  - BPCL SBI Octane card: Up to 25 reward points per ₹100 at BPCL outlets, plus 1% surcharge waiver

CONTACTLESS PAYMENTS (NFC TAP-TO-PAY)
All new Indian credit cards are NFC-enabled. Features:
- Tap and pay at compatible POS terminals
- Without PIN: Transactions up to ₹5,000 (RBI increased from ₹2,000 in 2020)
- Above ₹5,000: Card insert + PIN required
- Faster than insert/swipe + sign
- Same fraud protection as chip cards
- Disable contactless via app if not needed

TOKENISATION
RBI mandated card tokenisation effective October 2022:
- Card details (16-digit number, CVV, expiry) are replaced with a token at merchant sites
- The actual card number is not stored by merchants
- Tokenisation is per-card-per-merchant
- You consent to tokenisation during checkout; merchant stores token, not card
- Reduces fraud risk substantially in case of merchant data breach
- Tokenisation is FREE for customers

VIRTUAL CREDIT CARDS
Some banks offer virtual cards — instant digital credit cards usable for online transactions:
- HDFC NetSafe: One-time virtual card with set amount and expiry — for risky online merchants
- Axis NEO: Digital-first card with full virtual + physical
- SBI YONO Pay: Virtual card via app
Useful for security: even if merchant is breached, virtual card limits damage to that single transaction or short timeframe.

REWARD POINTS REDEMPTION OPTIONS
1. Statement credit: Points converted to cash credit on your statement (most flexible)
2. Catalogue: Mobile phones, appliances, vouchers (Amazon, Flipkart), travel
3. AirMiles: Convert to airline frequent flyer programs (e.g., 4 HDFC points = 1 Vistara point)
4. Travel: Flights, hotels via bank's travel portal — usually 1 point = ₹0.20-₹0.30
5. Gift cards: Multi-brand vouchers
6. Charity: Donate points to NGOs (some cards offer 1:1 ratio for charity)

MILESTONE BENEFITS
Many cards reward spending milestones:
- Quarterly: ₹50,000-₹1.5 lakh spends → bonus points, voucher, or fee waiver
- Annual: ₹3-5 lakh spends → premium voucher, lounge upgrade, fee waiver
- Lifetime: Some cards offer milestone rewards for being a customer for 5/10+ years

CO-BRANDED CARDS — DEEPER DIVE
Co-branded cards offer accelerated rewards or loyalty benefits within the partner's ecosystem:
- Amazon Pay ICICI: 5% cashback on Amazon (for Prime members), 2% on Amazon Pay partner merchants
- Flipkart Axis: 5% cashback on Flipkart, 4% on partner merchants
- Swiggy HDFC: 10% cashback on Swiggy
- Tata Neu HDFC: NeuCoins on Tata brands (BigBasket, Croma, IHCL, Air India, etc.)
- Vistara SBI Signature: 4 CV Points per ₹200 + class upgrade voucher
- IndiGo HDFC: 6X 6E rewards on IndiGo bookings

These cards make sense if your spending naturally aligns with the partner; otherwise a generic high-rewards card may be better.

REWARD CAPS AND EXCLUSIONS
Most cards have caps on accelerated rewards:
- "5X rewards on dining" might be capped at 2,500 points per month
- Categories often excluded: rent, fuel (depends), wallet loads, government, utilities, insurance, EMI, cash advance
- Many premium cards reduced caps in 2023-24 — Axis Magnus reduced earning structure significantly; HDFC Infinia, ICICI changed rewards on insurance/government

TIPS TO MAXIMISE CREDIT CARD VALUE
1. Match card to your spending: high travel → travel rewards; high online shopping → cashback or Amazon/Flipkart card
2. Use multiple cards strategically — e.g., one for online (Amazon Pay), one for offline (Axis Ace), one for travel (Vistara SBI)
3. Hit annual fee waiver thresholds
4. Redeem reward points before they expire (typically 2-3 years)
5. Use lounge access actively if you travel even occasionally
6. Always pay in full to keep rewards net-positive
7. Set up auto-pay for total amount due
8. Track spends via bank's app and SMS alerts
9. Watch for category caps and reward exclusions
10. Maintain CIBIL 750+ for the best premium card eligibility

Rates may change, verify with bank — check your card's MITC, rewards catalogue, and benefits page on the bank's website.
