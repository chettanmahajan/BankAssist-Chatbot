BUSINESS LOAN AND MSME FINANCE — COMPLETE GUIDE

OVERVIEW
Business loans in India are credit facilities offered to proprietors, partnership firms, LLPs, private limited companies, MSMEs, and large corporates for working capital, expansion, machinery purchase, infrastructure, or any business-related purpose. The Indian banking ecosystem offers a wide variety of business loan products under RBI's regulatory framework and various government schemes. Major lenders include SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Axis Bank, Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank, Yes Bank, Kotak, Bajaj Finserv, Tata Capital, Lendingkart, and other NBFCs. Rates and terms are indicative; rates may change, verify with bank.

TYPES OF BUSINESS LOANS

1. Term Loan: Lump sum disbursal repayable over fixed tenure (3-15 years) for capital expenditure — machinery, equipment, factory construction, fit-outs. Repayment via EMIs.

2. Working Capital Loan: Funds for day-to-day operations — inventory purchase, salary, rent, utilities. Forms include:
   a. Cash Credit (CC): Sanctioned limit against hypothecation of stocks and book debts. Interest on amount used.
   b. Overdraft (OD): Similar to CC but against collateral like FDs, property.
   c. Bill Discounting: Bank pays you upfront on customer invoices, recovers from customer at due date.
   d. Letter of Credit (LC): Bank guarantees payment to supplier on your behalf.
   e. Bank Guarantee (BG): Bank guarantees performance/payment to a counterparty.

3. Equipment Finance: Secured loan against the equipment being purchased. LTV up to 90%; tenure 3-7 years.

4. Loan Against Property (LAP): Secured loan pledging commercial/residential property. LTV up to 65%-70%, longer tenure (15 years), lower rates.

5. Invoice Financing / Factoring: Sale of receivables to a factor for immediate cash, factor collects from buyer.

6. Trade Finance: For import/export businesses — pre-shipment credit, post-shipment credit, LC, BG, packing credit.

7. Startup Funding: Specialised products for startups including Stand-Up India, MUDRA, Startup India seed fund.

INTEREST RATES
Business loan rates range from 8.5% per annum to 24% per annum:
- Secured loans (LAP, equipment): 9%-12% p.a.
- MSME working capital (CC/OD): 9.5%-14% p.a.
- Unsecured business loans (NBFC): 14%-24% p.a.
- Government scheme loans (MUDRA, CGTMSE): 8%-13%
Rates depend on credit profile, business vintage, financials, collateral, and the loan product. Public sector banks offer the lowest rates; private banks faster service; NBFCs the easiest approval but highest rates. Rates may change, verify with bank.

MSME — MICRO, SMALL, AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES

The Government of India classifies MSMEs as follows (revised 2020 definition, both manufacturing and services treated alike):
- Micro: Investment up to ₹1 crore AND turnover up to ₹5 crore
- Small: Investment up to ₹10 crore AND turnover up to ₹50 crore
- Medium: Investment up to ₹50 crore AND turnover up to ₹250 crore

MSME status enables:
- Priority sector lending status with lenders
- Lower interest rates and subsidised schemes
- Mandated 45-day payment from large buyers (MSMED Act, Section 15)
- TReDS (Trade Receivables Discounting System) access
- Subsidies on patent registration, ISO certification, technology upgrade
- Public procurement preference

UDYAM REGISTRATION
All MSMEs must register at udyamregistration.gov.in. Registration is free, Aadhaar-based, and grants an Udyam Registration Number (URN) for accessing schemes. Earlier called Udyog Aadhaar (UAM).

PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING (PSL)
RBI mandates that all banks must lend at least 40% of their Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) to priority sectors, including MSMEs, agriculture, education, housing, social infrastructure, renewable energy. Within PSL:
- Micro enterprises: 7.5% sub-target
- Small and marginal farmers: 9.5% sub-target
- Weaker sections: 11.5% sub-target
This ensures formal credit access for small businesses. Foreign banks with 20+ branches also follow PSL norms.

MUDRA YOJANA (Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana — PMMY)
Launched in 2015, MUDRA provides loans to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises. Categories:
- Shishu: Up to ₹50,000 (for very small businesses)
- Kishore: ₹50,001 to ₹5 lakh (for established small businesses)
- Tarun: ₹5 lakh to ₹10 lakh (for growth-stage businesses)
- Tarun Plus (introduced FY24-25): ₹10 lakh to ₹20 lakh

Features:
- No collateral required
- Backed by Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE)
- Interest rate 9.5%-12% (varies by lender)
- Tenure: 3-7 years
- Disbursed through banks, NBFCs, and MFIs
- Available for trading, services, manufacturing, and allied activities — excludes agriculture and corporate forms

CGTMSE — CREDIT GUARANTEE FUND TRUST FOR MICRO AND SMALL ENTERPRISES
A trust set up by GoI and SIDBI that provides credit guarantee to lenders for unsecured loans up to ₹5 crore (revised up from ₹2 crore) to micro and small enterprises. This means:
- Lender does not insist on collateral
- If borrower defaults, the trust pays up to 75%-85% of the outstanding to the lender
- Borrower's exposure is unchanged, but lender's risk is mitigated
- One-time guarantee fee 0.37%-2% of loan amount + annual service fee 0.55%-1.4%
This scheme massively expands credit access for small businesses without collateral.

STAND-UP INDIA SCHEME
A scheme to promote entrepreneurship among Scheduled Caste / Scheduled Tribe (SC/ST) and women entrepreneurs. Features:
- Loan ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore
- For greenfield enterprise in manufacturing, services, trading, agriculture-allied sectors
- At least one borrower per bank branch from SC/ST or women category
- Composite loan (term loan + working capital)
- Tenure 7 years, moratorium up to 18 months
- Interest at lowest applicable rate
- Mandatory hand-holding support and capacity building
Apply via standupmitra.in or bank branches.

PSB LOANS IN 59 MINUTES (PSBLOANSIN59MINUTES.COM)
A portal that provides in-principle approval for MSME loans up to ₹5 crore within 59 minutes. Uses GST, ITR, bank statement integration via APIs. After in-principle approval, lender completes due diligence and disburses typically within 7-8 working days. Loans available from 21 public and private sector banks.

ATMANIRBHAR BHARAT — ECLGS (Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme)
Launched during COVID-19 for MSMEs. Provides 20% additional working capital to existing MSME borrowers with 100% guarantee from NCGTC. Scheme has been extended and modified multiple times. Check current status.

ELIGIBILITY FOR BUSINESS LOANS
- Business vintage: 1-3 years for most loans (newer schemes for startups available)
- Turnover: ₹10 lakh minimum to ₹40 lakh, varies by lender
- Profitability: Net profit positive for last 1-2 years (some unsecured products waive)
- Credit score: Promoter CIBIL 700+, company CIBIL MSME score
- KYC: Business and promoter KYC documents
- Tax compliance: GST returns, ITR filing up to date

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED
Business documents:
- Business registration (Shop Establishment Act, partnership deed, MOA/AOA, Udyam certificate)
- GST registration certificate
- PAN of business and promoter
- Business address proof
- Trade licence, FSSAI, drug licence as applicable

Financial documents:
- Audited balance sheet and P&L for last 2-3 years
- ITR with computation for last 2-3 years
- Last 12 months' GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B)
- Last 6-12 months' bank statements of business account
- Cash flow projections (for working capital and large term loans)

Project/asset documents:
- Quotation/invoice of asset (for term loan/equipment)
- Property documents (for LAP)
- Stock and debtors statement (for CC limit)

KEY METRICS BANKS EVALUATE
- DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): Net operating income / debt service obligations. Should be 1.5+ for term loans.
- Current Ratio: Current assets / current liabilities. Should be 1.33+.
- Debt-Equity Ratio: Total debt / equity. Should be below 3:1 for MSMEs.
- Interest Coverage Ratio: EBIT / interest expense. Should be 2+ minimum.
- TOL/TNW: Total outside liabilities / tangible net worth. Should be below 4:1.

WORKING CAPITAL ASSESSMENT METHODS
Bank decides working capital limit based on:
1. Operating Cycle Method: For traders — limit = avg. holding period of stock + debtor days − creditor days, expressed in monthly turnover
2. Turnover Method: 20%-25% of projected annual turnover (used for limits up to ₹5 crore for MSMEs)
3. Tandon/Chore Committee Method: For larger limits, detailed assessment of working capital gap
4. Cash Budget Method: For seasonal businesses

PROCESSING FEE
- Public sector banks: 0.5%-1% of loan amount
- Private banks: 1%-2%
- NBFCs: 1%-3%
- Plus GST 18%
Other charges: legal, valuation, stamp duty, CERSAI, documentation.

COLLATERAL
- Loans under MUDRA Tarun, CGTMSE-backed loans, and certain government schemes: NO collateral
- Working capital limits up to ₹10 lakh under PSL: Often no collateral (stocks and debtors hypothecated)
- Above ₹10 lakh: Collateral typically required — property, FD, machinery, shares, life insurance
- LTV for LAP: 50%-70%
- LTV for machinery: 75%-85%

REPAYMENT
- Term loans: Equal monthly installments over tenure (3-15 years)
- Working capital: Renewed annually after review
- Bullet repayment: Lump sum at end (rare, for very short-term)
- Moratorium: 3-18 months for new projects until cash flow generation

GST AND TAX
- Interest on business loan is tax-deductible as business expense under Section 36(1)(iii)
- Processing fees and ancillary charges are also deductible
- GST 18% on processing fee and other service charges

GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
- First approach branch manager
- Escalate to bank's Internal Ombudsman / Nodal Officer
- File complaint at RBI's Banking Ombudsman via cms.rbi.org.in
- For MSME-specific issues: MSME Samadhaan portal for delayed payments
- Stand-Up India helpline for SC/ST and women entrepreneurs

TIPS FOR SUCCESSFUL APPLICATIONS
1. Maintain clean books with regular GST and ITR filing
2. Keep business and personal finances separate
3. Build banking relationship — keep balances in business account, route transactions through it
4. Don't apply to too many banks simultaneously
5. Have a clear, realistic business plan for term loans
6. Explore subsidised government schemes first (MUDRA, CGTMSE, Stand-Up India)
7. Negotiate processing fees and ROI based on competing offers
8. Read the sanction letter completely — note all charges, covenants, and conditions
9. Ensure DSCR is healthy before taking large term loans

Rates may change, verify with bank for current offers, scheme eligibility, and detailed terms.
